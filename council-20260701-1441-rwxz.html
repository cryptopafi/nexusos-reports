Substantive Answer

Strategic verdict: REVISE, then implement in phases.

The proposed RUO pricing-control model is the right strategic direction. Keep one VAT-inclusive public list price per SKU, back out VAT internally, calculate true SKU economics, and use the minimum of global, SKU, stack, customer-file, campaign, and channel caps. The 2026-07-01 evidence that only 4 of 30 SKUs retain at least 50% margin after VAT, COGS, and a full 30% marketing budget proves that the 30% pool must be a ceiling, not default spend.

Required modifications before implementation:

1. Derive SKU and stack caps from margin math, not manual optimism: max marketing pct = max(0, pre-marketing margin minus target post-marketing margin), then clamp by global 30% and all other caps.

2. Make VAT jurisdictional, versioned, and snapshotted per order line. Do not hardcode 20% or 21%.

3. Recompute VAT and net revenue after gross discounts. Discounts reduce the VAT base; paid CAC and direct marketing do not.

4. Separate accounting treatment from operational cap use. Customer discounts are contra-revenue. Promoter commissions may be contra-revenue if structured as a price reduction, or selling expense if paid out after sale. Paid ads and direct marketing are expenses. All still consume the same contribution-protection budget.

5. Add fail-closed gate enforcement at order line level. Missing VAT, cost, FX, stack, customer, campaign, or promoter eligibility should default to direct sale with no discount, no commission, and no paid CAC allocation.

6. Add an absolute EUR contribution floor as Response A recommends, because percentage margin alone can permit weak low-ASP SKUs such as Kisspeptin at EUR 41 to pass while producing insufficient cash contribution.

7. Version all cost, cap, class, and override decisions with audit logs and approval owners, as Response C emphasizes.

Recommended margin gates:

Use tiered gates, not one universal 50% or 60% gate. Final synthesis:

  • Direct retail sale: minimum 50% post-cost contribution margin, with controlled exceptions only by approval. Response C’s 40% direct gate is too permissive for this SKU set given the 4-of-30 evidence.
  • Small customer discount: 55% target after discount.
  • Promoter or affiliate sale: 55-60% post-marketing margin, depending on promoter risk and chargeback history.
  • Paid ads sale: 60-65% post-marketing margin because CAC and attribution volatility are real cash risks.
  • Stack or pack: 55-60% blended basket margin, with no weak component hidden without explicit approval and nightly or scheduled revalidation.
  • B2B quote: manual approval, normally 50-55% minimum unless volume, payment, and risk terms justify lower.
  • Internal/team: non-commercial class, tracked separately.

Marketing-budget classes:

  • Class A: 20-30% computed room. Eligible for controlled discounts, promoter tests, and paid-ad tests if the channel gate also passes.
  • Class B: 10-20% computed room. Eligible for moderate discount or promoter use; paid ads only with tight CAC cap.
  • Class C: 1-10% computed room. Manual approval, restricted campaigns, pack/sourcing track, or retention-only use.
  • Class D: 0%. No discount, no commission, no paid ads; direct sale only.

Named SKU policy:

  • Retatrutide stays at EUR 119.99 until the larger order and premium COA/safety-testing layer are ready.
  • Kisspeptin at EUR 41 is Class D unless repriced.
  • 5-Amino, Epithalon, MOTS-c, and TB-500 should move into pack architecture, sourcing improvement, or restricted marketing rather than broad public list cuts.

Minimum data model:

  • vat_jurisdictions or vat_rates: jurisdiction, rate, effective_from, effective_to, source, active.
  • fx_rates: currency pair, rate, effective_at, source, buffer.
  • sku_master: SKU, name, status, public gross price, currency, stock location, regulatory flags, default price mode.
  • sku_cost_components: supplier cost, inbound freight, customs, COA/testing amortization, packaging, payment fee, fulfillment, refund reserve, chargeback reserve, replacement reserve, FX rate, FX buffer, effective_from.
  • sku_economics_snapshot: VAT rate, net revenue, true unit cost, pre-marketing margin, max marketing pct at target floors, eligibility class, calculated_at.
  • marketing_caps: scope type, scope id, cap pct of net revenue, dates, approval owner, reason, active.
  • stack_definitions and stack_components: pack price, component SKUs, quantities, stack economics.
  • customer_files: customer, source type, promoter, approved discount code, max discount, max commission, max total marketing, eligible SKUs/stacks, owner, dates.
  • promoters: promoter id, status, commission treatment, default commission, eligible SKUs/stacks, risk flags.
  • campaigns_channels: campaign, channel, CAC cap, direct marketing cap, attribution rules, eligible SKUs/stacks, owner, dates.
  • order_line_gate_results: gross price, discount, VAT rate, net revenue, cost snapshot, commission, CAC, direct marketing, allowed marketing pct, binding cap, contribution EUR, contribution pct, pass/fail, failure reason.
  • cap_audit_log and approvals_audit_log: immutable record of cap, class, override, and approval changes.

Implementation order:

1. Freeze broad repricing, broad discounts, promoter rollout, and paid ads. Preserve one public VAT-inclusive list price.

2. Build VAT jurisdiction and FX snapshots, then populate true cost components for all 30 SKUs.

3. Generate SKU economics snapshots and classify A/B/C/D from computed data, with named policy overrides for Retatrutide, Kisspeptin, 5-Amino, Epithalon, MOTS-c, and TB-500.

4. Build calculation engine with regression tests for VAT extraction, post-discount VAT recompute, true unit cost, cap derivation, min-of-caps, absolute EUR floor, and fail-closed behavior.

5. Run gate in read-only/advisory mode against historical and simulated orders, including full 30%, stacked discount plus commission, missing VAT, stale FX, weak SKU in stack, and expired campaign cases.

6. Enforce transaction-level gates for direct sale and customer discounts on Class A/B only.

7. Add promoter alpha with allowlisted SKUs, trusted promoters, short effective dates, audit logs, and chargeback monitoring.

8. Add paid ads only for SKUs or stacks clearing 60-65% under real CAC data.

9. Add stack and pack expansion for weak high-sticker SKUs only after stack P&L revalidation works.

10. Add monthly governance to refresh costs, FX, caps, class assignments, and overrides.

Decision Pafi should make now:

Approve the architecture direction, but require these revisions before rollout. Do not allow universal 30% marketing allocation. Do not scale promoters, customer discounts, or paid ads until SKU economics, jurisdictional VAT, SKU classes, transaction-level gates, and audit logs are live.

Where Models Disagree

The main disagreement is margin strictness for direct sales and stacks. Response A recommends 50% direct, 55% promoter, and 58-60% paid ads. Response B recommends 50% floor and 60% commercial target, with 60-65% for paid ads. Response C allows 40% direct and 50% stack/bundle margin, with individual components allowed to dip to 35% if blended margin works. Given the brief’s weak SKU evidence, the synthesis rejects Response C’s 40% direct default as too loose.

The second disagreement is emphasis. Response A prioritizes margin-floor binding, absolute EUR contribution floors, and VAT-on-discount recomputation. Response B prioritizes standardized cap basis, fail-closed transaction logic, and regression testing. Response C prioritizes race conditions, governance, versioning, and dynamic cap review. These are complementary rather than mutually exclusive.

The third disagreement is accounting treatment nuance. Response A distinguishes promoter commissions as contra-revenue when structured as price reductions versus expense when paid out. Response B classifies customer discounts as contra-revenue and promoter commissions, paid ads, and direct marketing as expenses. The synthesis adopts Response A’s more precise treatment while preserving Response B’s operational point that all categories consume the shared cap.

Final Synthesis Trace

The final verdict follows directly from unanimous REVISE advisor positions. There is no PASS or BLOCK dissent: all three responses approve the strategic direction while identifying required changes before live scale. The strongest shared case is that public VAT-inclusive price stability plus internal SKU economics and min-of-caps control is the correct architecture for RUO ecommerce. The strongest shared risk is that without transaction-level enforcement and computed SKU caps, the system could still apply discounts, commissions, or CAC to SKUs that cannot support them.

The synthesis gives extra weight to Response A on contribution-floor math and VAT-on-discount recomputation, Response B on cap basis, fail-closed behavior, and test sequencing, and Response C on race conditions, governance, and dynamic cap refresh. The main substantive split is margin strictness. Because the brief’s own evidence shows severe margin weakness, the final recommendation rejects Response C’s 40% direct gate as a default and uses a stricter 50% direct floor, 55-60% promoter gate, and 60-65% paid-ads gate.

The final decision is therefore: approve the architecture in principle, revise the implementation spec, freeze risky SKU exceptions, build the data foundation and enforcement engine first, and delay promoter, discount, and paid-ad scale until gates, tests, audit logs, and governance are live.
