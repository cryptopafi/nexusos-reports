3 · STEP 1 — Corrective protocol (tied to actual findings)

Each item maps to a flagged result. Lead with the non-peptide fix the labs actually demand.

1.1 — Vitamin D repletion (supplement, not a peptide — but the #1 thing the labs require)

  • D3 (cholecalciferol) ~5,000 IU/day + K2 (MK-7) 100–200 µg/day, with fat, 8–12 weeks → re-measure; maintenance ~2,000–4,000 IU/day to hold 40–60 ng/mL.
  • Safety gate: draw PTH + ionized calcium before high-dose repletion.
💬 In plain words: A daily vitamin D pill paired with vitamin K2 (which steers the calcium into your bones, not your arteries). Re-test after ~2–3 months, then take a smaller maintenance dose.

1.2 — CJC-1295 (no-DAC) + Ipamorelin — growth-hormone (GH) secretagogue pair

Addresses age-related GH/IGF-1 decline driving the menopausal shift in body composition, sleep depth, recovery, skin and bone density. Bachmeyer prefers GH secretagogues over injected HGH.

  • CJC-1295 no-DAC ~100 µg + Ipamorelin ~100–200 µg SC, nightly pre-sleep, empty stomach.
  • Mandatory gate: draw IGF-1 first; re-check at 8–12 weeks.
💬 In plain words: Two small nightly injections that gently nudge your own body to release more of its natural "repair and renew" growth hormone while you sleep — rather than injecting the hormone itself. People notice better sleep, recovery, body shape, skin and bone strength.

1.3 — MOTS-c — mitochondrial / metabolic

Supports the menopausal metabolic transition. Carries into the longevity stack (§4 Pillar B).

  • ~0.25–0.5 mg/day SC, cycled; take with carbohydrates to avoid low blood sugar. Monitor fasting insulin + HOMA-IR.
💬 In plain words: A natural signal that tells your cells to burn fuel efficiently — like a metabolism tune-up for steadier energy. Take it with a little food so your blood sugar doesn't dip.

1.4 — Thymosin Alpha-1 — immune (conditional)

Only if a repeat CBC confirms persistently low neutrophils or recurrent infections. Monitor: CBC, symptom-led.

💬 In plain words: An "immune-system trainer" that keeps your defenses sharp. Only worth adding if a repeat blood test shows your infection-fighting cells stay low.
What we deliberately do NOT do for Step 1:
  • No HRT (hormone replacement therapy) push — menopause treated as metabolic/lifestyle first; estradiol still relatively preserved. HRT is a physician call if symptoms warrant.
  • No statin / LDL intervention — lipids clean, insulin-sensitive.
  • No aggressive DHEA manipulation — only marginally high.
💬 In plain words: We're not rushing into hormone replacement, cholesterol pills, or fiddling with DHEA — your numbers don't justify any of those right now.

4 · STEP 2 — Full longevity protocol

A complete healthspan stack organized by the biological systems that age. The thesis: most age-related decline traces to three core failures — long-term inflammation, insulin resistance, and a shortage of cellular energy (ATP) — plus shortening "cell clocks", a falling repair-hormone level, and a weakening immune system. Each pillar targets one. This is a menu, not an "all-at-once" plan — see the phased rollout at the end.

💬 In plain words: Step 2 is the optional "age well" plan. Think of aging as a few systems slowly running down — your cellular clock, your energy "batteries", your repair hormones, your immune guards and your skin. Each pillar below props up one of them. You don't start everything at once; you phase it in.

Pillar A — Telomere / cellular senescence · Epitalon (Epithalon)

Synthetic pineal tetrapeptide; the flagship longevity tool. Activates telomerase to slow telomere attrition — the root "clock" of cellular aging.

  • ~5–10 mg/day SC, 10–20 consecutive days, cycled every 3–6 months (Russian / Khavinson protocol).
  • Evidence honesty: human data limited; mechanism plausible, disease-reversal claims unsubstantiated. Experimental, physician-supervised.
💬 In plain words: Your cells have protective end-caps (like the plastic tip on a shoelace) that fray a little each time a cell divides. Epitalon aims to slow that fraying — the idea being to keep cells "younger" for longer. Done in short bursts a few times a year. The science is promising but still early.

Pillar B — Mitochondrial energy (the engine)

B1 · MOTS-c — metabolic reset

~0.25–0.5 mg/day SC, cycled, with carbs. Insulin sensitization + AMPK activation. Rule: MOTS-c first as the reset, SS-31 second.

💬 In plain words: The "metabolism tune-up" from Step 1 — it primes your cells before the next step.

B2 · SS-31 (Elamipretide) — inner-battery repair

Binds cardiolipin in the inner mitochondrial membrane, stabilizing the energy machinery and cutting damaging free-radical leakage. Run after the MOTS-c reset.

  • ~5 mg/day SC, cycled (4–8 week blocks) — exact dose physician-set. Symptom-led.
💬 In plain words: A repair tool for your mitochondria — the tiny "batteries" inside every cell. It helps them hold their charge, so you feel more energy and recover better.

B3 · NAD+ axis — 5-Amino-1MQ and/or NAD+

5-Amino-1MQ raises intracellular NAD+ and activates sirtuins / PGC-1α / AMPK. Subcutaneous injection only (we do not offer the oral form). The injectable is ~7–10× more potent than oral, so the dose is far smaller: titrate up from a low start (~2–5 mg/day SC), physician-set — and cycle a maximum of 90 days, then break (it loses effect fast through receptor attenuation). Optional direct NAD+ ~50–100 mg SC. Monitor: NAD+ level.

💬 In plain words: Helps your cells keep their energy "fuel currency" (NAD+) topped up — like recharging the batteries from another angle. It's a small daily injection (not a pill), and you run it for up to 3 months at a time, then take a break, because the body stops responding if you use it non-stop.

Pillar C — Growth-hormone axis (decline reversal) · pick ONE strategy

Reverses the age-related fall in growth hormone / IGF-1 (body comp, bone, sleep, skin, cognition). Exogenous HGH is rejected (pituitary shutdown, insulin resistance).

  • Option 1 (default, from §1.2): CJC-1295 no-DAC + Ipamorelin — 100 µg + 100–200 µg nightly SC.
  • Option 2: Tesamorelin — ~1–2 mg/day SC — stronger on belly fat + brain fog.
  • GATE: draw IGF-1 first. Do not run two GH strategies at once.
💬 In plain words: Both options coax your own body to make more of its natural "repair and renew" hormone (we don't inject the hormone itself). Pick one: the nightly pair is the all-rounder; Tesamorelin is the choice if stubborn belly fat or foggy thinking bother you most.

Pillar D — Systemic repair & anti-inflammatory

D1 · BPC-157 — the daily base

~250–500 µg/day SC. Gut lining, tendon/joint, vascular, broad anti-inflammatory. Injection only (oral ineffective). Monitor: hs-CRP.

💬 In plain words: A "body repair kit" — supports healing in the gut, joints, tendons and blood vessels, and calms inflammation. A small daily injection.

D2 · TB-500 — cyclical tissue/systemic

~2.5–5 mg every ~5 days, cycled on/off. Pairs with BPC-157 for connective-tissue and cardiac/vascular repair. Inject separately.

💬 In plain words: BPC-157's recovery partner — helps muscles, tissues and the heart repair. Used in on-and-off cycles, not every day.

Pillar E — Immune resilience · Thymosin Alpha-1

Rebuilds T-cell-mediated immunity that declines with age — relevant given the borderline-low neutrophils. ~1.5 mg SC ~2×/week, cycled. Monitor: CBC.

💬 In plain words: An "immune-system trainer" that keeps your defenses sharp as they naturally weaken with age. Twice-a-week injection.

Pillar F — Skin / gene-expression reset · GHK-Cu

Copper tripeptide; drives collagen, skin quality, wound and tissue repair and a "youthful" gene-expression pattern. ~1–2 mg/day SC. Transient injection-site redness is benign.

💬 In plain words: A "skin and renewal" copper peptide — supports collagen, smoother skin and wound healing, and switches on more youthful cell behavior. A little redness at the injection spot is normal.

Pillar G — Sleep / HPA recalibration · DSIP (conditional)

DSIP = Delta Sleep-Inducing Peptide. Calms the stress (HPA) axis and restores deep sleep — the foundation every other longevity lever depends on, often disrupted in menopause. Add only if sleep is poor. ~100–250 µg SC pre-sleep, symptom-led.

💬 In plain words: A "deep-sleep helper" that quiets the stress system so you get the deep, restorative sleep everything else depends on. Only add it if you're sleeping badly.

Foundation — non-peptide supplements (the bedrock)

  • Vitamin D3 + K2 — corrective + permanent (see §1.1).
  • Magnesium ~300–400 mg/day — vitamin-D cofactor, sleep, glucose.
  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA) ~2–3 g/day — anti-inflammatory, heart, brain.
  • Creatine monohydrate ~3–5 g/day — muscle, bone, and brain in post-menopausal women.
  • Folate: reduce/stop the folic-acid supplement (yours is high).
💬 In plain words: The everyday basics that make everything else work better — vitamin D + K2, magnesium, fish oil and creatine (great for muscle, bone and memory after menopause). And ease off the folic-acid supplement, since your level is already high.
Recommended phased rollout (physician-gated — don't start everything at once):
  1. Phase 0 — Foundation (weeks 0–4): D3+K2, magnesium, omega-3, creatine. Draw all missing labs.
  2. Phase 1 — Daily base (week 2+): BPC-157 + GHK-Cu. Start an Epitalon block anytime.
  3. Phase 2 — Mitochondrial (week 4–6): MOTS-c reset → SS-31; add 5-Amino-1MQ/NAD+ if energy lags.
  4. Phase 3 — GH axis (after IGF-1 baseline): CJC-1295 no-DAC + Ipamorelin (or Tesamorelin).
  5. Phase 4 — As-needed: Thymosin Alpha-1, TB-500, DSIP.
💬 In plain words: Build it up in stages — vitamins and lab tests first, the gentle daily peptides next, then energy and hormone support, and the optional extras last. Your doctor guides each step.
