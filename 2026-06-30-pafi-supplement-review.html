PER-SUPPLEMENT VERDICTS

1. ActivLab Arginine 3 (L-arginine, NO/“pump”) — NOT INDICATED (lean toward avoid)

HIS-data reason: 1. He is already driving the GH/IGF and vascular axis hard. IGF-1 is 240 (HIGH) even after pulling Tesamorelin/Ipamorelin. The whole point of an arginine NO-booster (vasodilation, GH nudge, “pump”) is redundant against a stack that just produced a high-IGF-1 readout. No incremental benefit to chase. 2. Rising hematocrit (47.2% and climbing on TRT). His blood is thickening from TRT. A nitric-oxide vasodilator on top is not dangerous per se, but it is treating a symptom (vascular tone) he doesn’t have a documented problem with, while the real cardiovascular variable on his chart (hematocrit + hs-CRP 1.57) is going the wrong way. Effort is misallocated. 3. No cardiovascular indication in his data. There is no BP reading, no endothelial-dysfunction marker, no erectile/vascular complaint on file that arginine would address. hs-CRP rose to 1.57 — that’s an inflammation signal, and oral L-arginine does not fix it. 4. Herpes/arginine–lysine caveat: no herpes/cold-sore history is in his record (searched — none found). So this is not an active contraindication, but it is the standard caution: if he is HSV-prone, high-dose arginine can favor viral replication (arginine:lysine ratio). Flag, not a block.

Bottom line: This is a performance/“pump” supplement with no anchor in his labs or goals. His goals (from the chart) are metabolic (glucose 110, HOMA 2.0) and hormonal optimization — arginine addresses neither. Skip it. If he specifically wants a training pump on gym days, it is low-risk at 3–6 g pre-workout, but understand it is cosmetic, not corrective.

Bottom line: Defensible for sleep/stress given his night-shift life, and the lowest-risk of the three — provided he (a) checks TSH/fT3/fT4 first (thyroid caveat) and (b) does NOT also run TribuZMA (B6/Zn stacking). Cycle it; don’t run KSM-66 indefinitely.

  • Monitoring: recheck TSH/fT3/fT4 at ~8 weeks; if TSH drops or fT4 climbs, stop.
