🎯 Decisions & Actionable Intel (2026-06-13)

  1. TRT — HOLD, do NOT reduce. (Delphi D3, EPR 17/20.) The "high" flag is the lab's conservative age-≥50 population ceiling, not a harm threshold. Trough 770 ng/dL is below AUA's 900 reduction trigger, within optimization ranges, and below Bachmeyer's 800–1,200 target (arguably under-treated by his lens). Free-T 21 is upper-normal for age. Hct fine. TRAVERSE (n=5,246) showed no MACE increase. No reduce, no escalate. Optional, symptom-driven only: micro-dose (50 mg q2d or 75 mg q3.5d) to lift trough toward 800–900 and flatten the day-2–3 peak (~1,100–1,400). Decisive variable = how you feel (energy/libido/mood).
    💬 In plain words: Your level is good. It only looks "high" against a cautious chart for 50-year-olds — every serious framework says it's fine, and Bachmeyer would call it slightly low. We hold the dose. The only optional tweak is splitting the weekly shot into smaller, more frequent doses for steadier levels — and only if you'd want that for how you feel.
  2. Tesamorelin — STOPPED (2026-06-13) + GH axis paused. IGF-1 240 + the glucose/HOMA drift trace to Tesamorelin (2 mg, 5-on/2-off — correct cadence; the 2 mg dose is what over-drove IGF-1; you're a brisk responder). GH is diabetogenic, and the AM fasting glucose is inflated if the draw followed an "on" night. Decision: stop Tesamorelin now; pause the whole GH axis (no CJC/Ipamorelin) 4–6 weeks; keep TRT + Retatrutide + MOTS-c running. Restart lever = drop to 1 mg, keep 5-on/2-off — only if IGF-1 back in range + glucose <100.
    💬 In plain words: The growth-hormone peptide pushed your IGF-1 above the healthy ceiling, which nudges blood sugar up. You did the schedule right — the dose was just strong for you. Stop it, rest the growth-hormone side for ~a month, retest clean, and if you restart, do half the dose.

  5. B12 → drop 2000 → 1000 mcg/day methyl-B12 (overshoot is harmless, just unneeded). B6/P5P stays stopped (homocysteine 7.92 is optimal). Methylation is fixed.
