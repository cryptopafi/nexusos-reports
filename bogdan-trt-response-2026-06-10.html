💬 Plain-Words Layer — what the red items mean

🔴 Testosterone & Free-T slightly over range

In plain words: The TRT did its job — maybe a touch too well. Your levels are now a little above the top of normal. That's not an emergency, but the fix is to nudge the dose down a little (once we confirm the blood was drawn right before your weekly shot), not up.

🔴 IGF-1 high (240)

In plain words: This is the growth-hormone peptides you just started doing their thing — but it's now above the healthy ceiling. Pushing it higher tends to raise blood sugar, so the move is to ease that dose back rather than chase a bigger number.

🔴 Fasting glucose 110 + 🟠 HOMA 2.0

In plain words: Your blood sugar and insulin are starting to creep up — early "pre-insulin-resistance." It's linked to the growth-hormone push above. The good news: the Retatrutide and MOTS-c already in your stack are made to fix exactly this — make sure they're running.

🟡 Hematocrit 47.2 (climbing)

In plain words: Testosterone thickens the blood. You're still in the safe zone but trending up, so we watch it closely, keep you hydrated, and absolutely don't raise the TRT dose. If it climbs near the top, a blood donation fixes it instantly.

🟠 B12 over range (935)

In plain words: Your March B12 was nearly deficient; now it's overcorrected to above-normal. Harmless, but you can cut back the B12 supplement.
