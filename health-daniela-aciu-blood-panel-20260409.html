P1

Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

Gut-Thyroid Axis Repair + Immune Modulation

Thymosin Alpha-1 (TA1)

PRIORITY: Autoimmune

Dose: 1.6mg subcutaneous, 2x/week

Rationale: Adaptogenic immune modulator. In autoimmune states (Anti-Tg 54.60, 1.82x limit), TA1 suppresses rather than stimulates immune activity via TLR/NF-kB signaling. Restores T-regulatory cell function, which is the key failure point in Hashimoto's (Bachmeyer: T-reg collapse from ATP depletion allows autoimmune attack to continue).

Source: Bachmeyer "Thymosin Alpha 1 Is A Powerhouse For Life" / Cortex health collection

BPC-157

Gut-Thyroid Axis

Dose: 250-500mcg subcutaneous, 2x/day

Rationale: Gut-thyroid axis healing. Bachmeyer states leaky gut (zonulin-mediated tight junction disruption leading to LPS-TLR4-NF-kB cascade) is present in 100% of Hashimoto's patients. BPC-157 is the primary gut healing peptide addressing systemic inflammation upstream of thyroid autoimmunity. Multi-mechanism: gastric mucosal protection, angiogenesis, tissue repair.

Route: Subcutaneous ONLY. Oral administration is pharmacologically inactive (hepatic first-pass + proteolytic degradation).

Source: 16 video sources in knowledge base / Bachmeyer "BPC157 Does Everything"

KPV

Anti-Inflammatory

Dose: 500mcg oral, 1x/day

Rationale: Alpha-MSH derived tripeptide. Powerful anti-inflammatory that suppresses cytokine storms. Works synergistically with BPC-157 for gut healing. Addresses the NF-kB inflammatory cascade that drives Anti-Tg antibody production. CRP at 1 mg/L is currently normal, but KPV targets organ-specific inflammation (thyroid) that CRP may not capture.

Source: Bachmeyer "KPV The Greatest Anti Inflammatory Ever" / 4 video sources
