1 Executive Summary

The agentic harness decision is the most consequential AI infrastructure choice organizations will make in 2026. Three paths exist: vendor-managed platforms (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft) deliver fastest time-to-production and automatic model velocity inheritance at the cost of accelerating consumption-based pricing and opaque data residency; open-source harnesses (LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen, Letta, Agno) offer multi-model portability and community velocity with significant operational complexity — framework choice alone shifts benchmark performance by up to 30 percentage points on identical models; and custom-built orchestration maximizes control and capability differentiation but requires ≥5 dedicated AI engineer FTEs, accumulates Agentic Technical Debt, and carries persistent Stochastic Tax even after optimization.

Across all time horizons, the regret-minimization strategy for most enterprise profiles is a deliberate hybrid: OSS orchestration layer (LangGraph or OpenAI Agents SDK) + vendor model APIs (swappable via MCP/A2A) + internally-owned evaluation and compliance plumbing — avoiding full platform lock-in while retaining frontier model velocity. The August 2, 2026 EU AI Act full applicability deadline is a hard forcing function for regulated EU organizations to eliminate vendor-managed platforms unless contractual conformity assurance and EU data residency are guaranteed.

Only 2% of enterprises had deployed agentic AI at scale by 2025, but 45% of Fortune 500 are actively piloting — placing the market at the inflection point. Organizations that choose harness architecture deliberately now will build sustainable competitive advantages; those that default to vendor convenience will face growing lock-in costs by 2028.

3 Key Findings

1

[1][3][6][9] Vendor platforms are shifting from flat-rate to consumption-based metering, inflating real TCO 3–10× at scale.

Anthropic split Agent SDK billing from chat subscriptions effective June 15, 2026 — the $20 Pro subscription no longer covers unlimited Agent SDK usage; credits are issued at $20/$100/$200 per tier [1][3]. OpenAI's Assistants API targets mid-2026 deprecation in favor of the metered Responses API [5][6]. Google ADK's Agent Engine charges $0.0864/vCPU-hour from December 2025 [7][8]. Microsoft Copilot Studio costs $200/month for 25,000 Copilot Credits, scaling to Agent CUs for Azure AI Foundry [9]. At >1M agent-turns/month, vendor platforms can cost 3–10× equivalent OSS deployments using commodity model APIs.

● HIGH CONFIDENCE
2

[11][12][13][14] OSS frameworks bifurcate sharply: LangGraph (production-grade) vs. CrewAI (rapid-prototype) — framework choice shifts performance by up to 30 percentage points on identical models.

LangGraph leads production reliability (9/10), cost efficiency ($0.08/task), and passed CrewAI in GitHub stars in early 2026 [11][14]. CrewAI delivers fastest time-to-production but carries 3× LangGraph's token overhead on simple tasks [13]. AutoGen (8/10 reliability) dominates code-execution scenarios but costs 5–6× LangGraph on open-ended reasoning [12][13]. LangChain retains ~85K GitHub stars but is considered a broader "application framework" rather than a dedicated agent harness [14]. Frontier models crossed 80% on SWE-bench Verified in late 2025/early 2026 — scaffold selection is now as important as model selection [15].

● HIGH CONFIDENCE
3

[28][29][31] Custom harnesses yield compounding strategic advantages over 3+ years via "Code as Agent Harness" — but accumulate Agentic Technical Debt with persistent Stochastic Tax.

The "Code as Agent Harness" paradigm (arXiv 2605.18747, May 2026) establishes code as the operational substrate for agent reasoning, action, environment modeling, and verification — enabling enterprise workflow differentiation [28]. AutoHarness (arXiv 2603.03329) demonstrates that custom code harnesses allow smaller models (Gemini-2.5-Flash) to outperform larger ones (Gemini-2.5-Pro) on structured tasks, at lower cost [29]. Agentic Technical Debt (arXiv 2605.27320) formalizes the structural risk: Stochastic Tax (recurring operating burden from probabilistic agents in business workflows) remains positive even when debt is minimized — it is a permanent operating cost, not a one-time buildout [31].

● HIGH CONFIDENCE (3 independent T1 academic sources)
4

[16][17][19][35] EU AI Act (August 2, 2026) and NIST AI Agent Standards Initiative (February 17, 2026) create divergent compliance postures — OSS self-hosted is the only architecture with full data sovereignty.

EU AI Act becomes fully applicable August 2, 2026, requiring high-risk AI systems in EU (employment, credit, education, law enforcement) to complete conformity assessments, register in the EU AI database, and implement quality management systems [16][17]. NIST formally launched the AI Agent Standards Initiative on February 17, 2026 [19]. The Cloud Security Alliance AAGATE architecture (December 22, 2025) provides the most concrete Kubernetes-native governance implementation [19]. For regulated industries, OSS self-hosted or custom-built is the only architecture where the enterprise retains full auditability, data residency, and conformity evidence ownership [35].

● HIGH CONFIDENCE (EU AI Act official text · NIST official · CSA research)
5

[21][22][38][40] MCP and A2A are converging as the de facto agent interoperability standards — donated to Linux Foundation, adopted by all major vendors; non-compliant harnesses face growing integration debt.

MCP (Anthropic, November 2024) reached 97 million downloads (from 100K at launch) [21]. A2A (Google, April 2025) launched with 50+ enterprise partners including Salesforce, Accenture, SAP, and Deloitte, reaching v1.0 in early 2026 [22]. Both protocols were donated to Linux Foundation governance and adopted by OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and Anthropic [21][38]. MCP provides tool/context integration; A2A enables agent-to-agent task delegation. An arXiv survey (2505.02279, 2025) maps the full interoperability protocol ecosystem: MCP + A2A + ACP + ANP [40].

● HIGH CONFIDENCE

[23][24][25] Enterprise adoption is at the inflection: 2% at scale, 45% F500 piloting — vendor platforms capture pilots but scale costs surprise finance teams.

Only 2% of enterprises had deployed agentic AI at scale by 2025; 45% of F500 are actively piloting, signaling an inflection from experimentation to mass adoption [23]. Salesforce Agentforce cut $100M in support costs handling 3M customer conversations — the most concrete enterprise ROI case study available [24]. However, full Agentforce 360 implementations run $300–500+/user/month effective cost when licensing, integrations, and Flex Credits are included [25]. Copilot Studio and Agentforce deploy pre-built use cases in 4–6 weeks but consumption-based pricing "can surprise finance teams" at scale.

● HIGH CONFIDENCE
7

[26][27][34][36][37] Security has become a strategic differentiator: prompt injection grew 340% YoY in 2025; vendor harnesses have unverifiable sandboxing; custom harnesses require full in-house security engineering.

Documented prompt injection attempts against enterprise AI systems grew 340% year-over-year in late 2025, with indirect attacks accounting for >55% of observed incidents [26]. A 2025 Omdia survey found 95% of 376 enterprise stakeholders prefer custom AI for control, but 91% acknowledge its complexity [34]. Accenture and PwC have adopted vendor-managed Claude for regulated environments (finance, life sciences) where Anthropic's contractual compliance posture is the key value proposition [36][37].

🏭 Vendor-Managed — Temporal Analysis

Short-Term (0–12 months)
Score: 45/60

What works today: Anthropic Claude on Bedrock/claude.ai (enterprise contracts, SOC2, HIPAA BAA available), OpenAI Responses API (GA), Google Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform, Microsoft Copilot Studio (4–6 week deployment). All four provide built-in rate limiting, guardrails, and logging. Strongest dimension: Time-to-Production (5/5) and Capability Velocity (5/5). Weakest: Lock-in Risk (2/5) and Multi-model Portability (2/5). Reference customers: Accenture (Anthropic), Salesforce (OpenAI), Sana/Workday (Microsoft).

Mid-Term (1–3 years)
Score: 42/60

Trajectory: Pricing models are compressing faster than feature gains. Anthropic's June 2026 billing split is a preview of accelerating metering. OpenAI's "Project Orchard" (commercial AutoGen successor) will blur vendor/OSS boundaries. Google ADK's multi-provider support signals strategic pivot. Microsoft integrating Claude into Azure AI Foundry ends pure-play OpenAI dominance. Roadmap velocity remains fastest of any class. TCO score falls to 2/5 as consumption billing matures and agent workloads scale.

Long-Term (3–7 years)
Score: 36/60

Survives commoditization? Vendor platforms will likely commoditize at the model layer but differentiate on compliance attestations, enterprise integrations (CRM, ERP, ITSM), and sovereign cloud deployments. EU AI Act Phase 2, sovereign-AI mandates, and on-prem renaissance will pressure all vendors to offer fully isolated deployments. Lock-in risk deteriorates to 1/5 — Vendor Durability is the key uncertainty (Foundation Model Transparency Index 2025: average score fell 58→40; acquisition risk is real).

🚨 Breaks-the-Thesis Events — Vendor-Managed
  • Acquisition + deprecation: If a major vendor (e.g., Anthropic acquired by a large tech company) deprecates current APIs within 18 months — forces emergency re-architecture. History: OpenAI deprecated davinci-001 with 6 months notice.
  • Pricing step-change: If per-turn token costs double (e.g., GPT-5.5-level reasoning models become default), agent workloads priced at $0.03/turn at 1M turns/day = $30K/day. Finance teams will re-evaluate overnight.
  • EU AI Act non-conformity ruling: If EU AI Office rules a major platform's agent runtime is non-conformant for high-risk use cases, enterprise customers in EU must migrate within 6–12 months or face fines up to €35M / 7% of global turnover.

🚨 Breaks-the-Thesis Events — Open-Source

⚙️ Custom-Built — Temporal Analysis

Short-Term (0–12 months)
Score: 38/60

What works today: "Code as Agent Harness" (arXiv 2605.18747) is now a validated paradigm with enterprise workflow applications in DevOps, GUI automation, scientific discovery. AutoHarness (arXiv 2603.03329) proves custom harnesses can beat larger models at lower cost. However: 6–18 months to first production deployment; requires ≥5 dedicated AI engineers; 2025 Omdia: 95% want control, 91% acknowledge complexity. Security posture starts low (2/5) — no pre-built guardrails; Agentic Technical Debt accumulates immediately.

Mid-Term (1–3 years)
Score: 48/60

Trajectory: Custom harnesses become competitive once the "Stochastic Tax" (arXiv 2605.27320) is accounted for in the architecture. Governed runtime evolution (arXiv 2605.27328: HarnessMutation framework with validation, traceability, rollback) provides the governance model. By year 2, a mature custom harness with MCP/A2A integration has maximum model portability (5/5) and the highest observability ceiling (5/5 — full telemetry ownership). TCO begins to win vs vendor platforms at this horizon.

Long-Term (3–7 years)
Score: 58/60

Survives commoditization? For AI-native product companies where agents are core IP — yes, and it's the only defensible moat. Custom harnesses score 5/5 on lock-in risk, compliance, vendor durability, multi-model portability, and interoperability. The "Code as Agent Harness" compounds: proprietary skill libraries, domain-specific evaluators, and trained routing logic become a capability differentiator that vendor platforms cannot replicate. The 1-point deficit vs OSS long-term is on Talent Leverage (3 vs 3 — both need engineers), but custom has a slight advantage in Observability and Security.

🚨 Breaks-the-Thesis Events — Custom-Built
  • Talent attrition: If key AI engineers leave before institutional knowledge is codified — the custom harness becomes an unmaintainable black box. Agentic Technical Debt converts to acute liability. Recovery: 6–12 month ramp for replacements.
  • Capability gap with frontier models: If vendor-managed platforms unlock capabilities (computer-use, long-context multimodal, advanced memory) that take 12–18 months to replicate internally, custom harnesses may fall behind frontier capability ceiling. The model velocity gap (2/5 short-term) is the custom harness's Achilles heel.
  • Regulatory complexity explosion: If EU AI Act Phase 2 (post-2028) introduces mandatory AI audit trails requiring specific artifact formats, custom harnesses may need complete audit layer re-builds — whereas vendors can amortize compliance engineering across their entire customer base.
