3a · DHEA-S & HPA Axis

Dr. Bachmeyer does NOT recommend supplementing DHEA directly (oral, cream, or injectable). He views low DHEA-S as a downstream symptom of a misfiring HPA axis. His protocol relies entirely on upstream repair to restore native DHEA production:

  • DSIP (Delta Sleep-Inducing Peptide): Primary HPA recalibration tool. Does not suppress cortisol — stabilizes and harmonizes it. Restores healthy morning cortisol peak and evening decline.
  • MOTS-c: Optimizes adrenal cellular environment to efficiently manufacture DHEA natively.
  • BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound 157): Modulates vagal tone, shifts autonomic nervous system from sympathetic into parasympathetic recovery. Directly calms HPA axis.
  • CAX (Semax/Selank): Restores hypothalamus-pituitary-adrenal communication. Tells hypothalamus to exit "panic emergency mode."
  • Morning sunlight: 20–30 minutes direct sun on face daily — resets cortisol and cortical rhythm.

3b · Tesamorelin / GH Peptide Sequencing

Bachmeyer's rule is absolute: GH (Growth Hormone) peptides cannot run concurrently with a misfiring HPA axis. With Cortisol AM at floor and DHEA-S at 88, the pituitary cannot respond effectively to Tesamorelin. His sequencing:

  1. Complete 20-day DSIP cycle first
  2. Reintroduce Tesamorelin ONLY after natural cortisol rhythm is restored (healthy morning peak + evening drop, confirmed subjectively by sleep quality + mental clarity + libido)
Current status: Tesamorelin correctly NOT in stack.

3c · Homocysteine / Methylation Support

Bachmeyer targets one-carbon metabolism directly:

  • Methylated B-vitamins + Riboflavin B2: Clinical-grade methylated B-complex + 80 mg of Vitamin B2 twice daily (160 mg/day total) — provides exact cofactors for the folate cycle
  • MOTS-c: Specifically boosts the folate cycle and optimizes one-carbon metabolism
  • 5-Amino-1MQ (5-Amino-1-Methylquinolinium): 50–100 mg daily. Blocks NNMT (Nicotinamide N-Methyltransferase) enzyme, preventing NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide) waste and metabolic roadblocks that elevate homocysteine

3d · Cardiovascular / CRP Reduction

Bachmeyer views CV (cardiovascular) disease as an inflammatory disease, not a cholesterol problem. Chronic inflammation damages endothelial lining → micro-tears → LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein) sent as "ambulance" → oxidized LDL → foam cells → plaque. His CV repair stack:

  • KPV (Lysine-Proline-Valine): Directly inhibits NF-κB (Nuclear Factor kappa-B) pathway, shuts down inflammatory cytokines, stops macrophages turning into foam cells. Dose: 250 mcg SubQ ×2/day. Cycle: 4–6 weeks ON, 2–4 weeks OFF. Note: the "52% CRP drop" and "11% calcification reduction" figures cited in earlier analysis are not indexed in human clinical literature — KPV's anti-inflammatory evidence base is primarily preclinical/inflammatory bowel models. Use KPV as anti-inflammatory adjunct, not as a calcification reversal agent.
  • BPC-157: Patches micro-tears in arterial endothelial lining. Dose: 500 mcg to 1 mg daily SubQ.
  • TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4): Cardiac tissue repair. Dose: 5 mg every 5 days SubQ. Loading: 5 mg ×2/week for first 4 weeks, then taper to 2.5 mg/week maintenance. Note: human RCT (Randomized Controlled Trial) evidence for myocardial fibrosis reversal is limited — primarily rodent post-MI (Myocardial Infarction) studies. Reframe as speculative regenerative adjunct.

3e · Retatrutide + MOTS-c Nutrition Rule

Mandatory: 100–200 g quality carbohydrates daily (rice, potatoes, fruit) while on Retatrutide + MOTS-c. Retatrutide's glucagon component burns fat via carbohydrate oxidation. Without carbs, it burns muscle. MOTS-c rapidly depletes glycogen stores — insufficient carb intake causes metabolic disaster.

5 · Statin Decision

Bachmeyer's position: CV disease is inflammatory, not cholesterol-driven. Statins deplete CoQ10 (Coenzyme Q10) → mitochondrial dysfunction. His approach: address inflammation with KPV + BPC-157 + TB-500, not lipid-lowering.

Clinical reality for Ehsan: Confirmed calcific plaque in LAD = documented coronary artery disease. AHA/ESC (American Heart Association / European Society of Cardiology) guidelines: high-intensity statin is Class I indication for confirmed calcific coronary plaque regardless of LDL level.

Verdict: Accept the statin — Bachmeyer's anti-statin position is defensible for primary prevention but is overridden by documented CAD (Coronary Artery Disease).

The two approaches are complementary, not antagonistic:

  • Statin → addresses lipids (guideline-mandated)
  • Bachmeyer stack (KPV + BPC-157 + TB-500) → addresses inflammation (root cause)

When statin starts: add Ubiquinol 200 mg/day (reduced form CoQ10, preferred for >40yo) — offsets statin-induced mitochondrial CoQ10 depletion.

Preferred statin: Rosuvastatin 20–40 mg/day (preferred over atorvastatin in fatty liver — less hepatic metabolism, lower drug interaction risk).

GATE: Do not initiate statin before lipid panel + ApoB (Apolipoprotein B) + Lp(a) (Lipoprotein-a) + ALT/AST/CK baseline.

6 · Independent Clinical Additions

These supplement Bachmeyer's recommendations where his protocol has evidence gaps or omissions. They do not replace any Bachmeyer recommendation.

A · Omega-3: Switch to EPA-Dominant Formulation

Icosapent ethyl (pure EPA — Eicosapentaenoic Acid) 4 g/day. REDUCE-IT trial demonstrated 25% reduction in MACE (Major Adverse Cardiovascular Events) in patients with elevated triglycerides + statin therapy. Mixed EPA/DHA (Docosahexaenoic Acid) — STRENGTH trial — failed. Given confirmed LAD plaque — EPA-dominant is the evidence-based choice.

B · Retatrutide Titration — Slow to Monthly Steps

Published Phase 2 protocol used 4-week titration intervals. In MASLD (Metabolic Dysfunction-Associated Steatotic Liver Disease), faster titration increases GI adverse events and dehydration-driven AKI (Acute Kidney Injury) risk. Slow to +0.5 mg every 4 weeks.

C · BPC-157 + TB-500 Cycling (VEGF Consideration)

Both BPC-157 and TB-500 upregulate VEGF (Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor) and pro-angiogenic pathways. In a patient with coronary plaque, indefinite concurrent open-ended use warrants a defined cycling schedule: BPC-157 5-on/2-off weekly cycle; TB-500 maximum 8-week course per cycle with 4-week break.

D · KPV Calcification Claim — Clarification

The "11% calcification reduction" figure cited in earlier analysis is not supported by indexed human literature. KPV's evidence base is anti-inflammatory (NF-κB, colitis models). Use as CRP adjunct — not as a calcification reversal therapy.

E · Consider TMG (Trimethylglycine) for Homocysteine

1–3 g/day. Acts as direct methyl donor, independent of MTHFR status. Complementary to the methylated B-stack. Recheck homocysteine at 12 weeks.

F · Check MTHFR Genotype

One-time genetic test. If MTHFR C677T homozygous — explains why normal B12/folate is not clearing homocysteine. Determines lifetime methylation strategy.
