01 · Executive Summary

The single broken row

The SolNest 2029 Stretch Scenario claims $227M total platform revenue, broken into five streams. Four of the five rows reconcile exactly with the stated inputs. The fifth row, Affiliate Hardware Checkout, has a narrative-to-number mismatch that a skeptical investor (or SEC §10b-5 reviewer) will catch in under 60 seconds by replaying the formula in the Assumptions box.

The slide states $520M GMV (modules + inverters ≈ 40% of $1.30/W × 2 GW system cost). The actual math gives $1.04B GMV, not $520M. If the narrative is taken literally, Row 3 revenue is $281M and total 2029 platform revenue is $367M, not $227M.

The fix is simple: add two implicit assumptions to the yellow callout box (A5 capture share + A6 checkout routing) and reword the Row 3 narrative. Fifteen minutes of editing closes the gap and makes the slide defensible.

F1 — Row 3 narrative vs number mismatch
Critical

The deck says $520M GMV = modules + inverters ≈ 40% of $1.30/W × 2 GW system cost. The math gives $2.6B × 40% = $1.04B GMV, not $520M. The $140M revenue only reconciles with a $520M GMV. If a hostile investor replays the formula they derive $367.7M total — which looks like either a 62% arithmetic error or number-engineering to hit a round target.

Fix

  • Add A6 — ~50% of installer volume routes through SolNest affiliate hardware checkout
  • Reword Row 3 narrative: "$520M GMV — modules + inverters on the ~1 GW that routes through SolNest checkout (50% × 2 GW × 40% × $1.30/W)"

High

09 · Investor Q&A

Pre-meeting Prep

Q1 — "Where does the 2 GW/yr anchor come from?"

4% share of 50 GW A1 market at steady state by year 4. Not all 2 GW are SolNest revenue, just the volume SolNest facilitates via installer partnerships.

Q2 — "Show me the math from your inputs to the $140M hardware line."

Without F1 fix, you cannot. With F1 fix: "We assume ~50% of installer volume routes through our affiliate checkout. So $1.30/W × 1 GW × 40% module+inverter share × 27% rev-share = $140M."

Q3 — "Industry conversion is ~15%. Why do you project 30%?"

"Stretch target. Business case holds at 15% because scans are 1% of revenue. Volume is the sensitivity."

Q4 — "50 kW avg roof is small for C&I."

"Fair. Our assumed blend of small commercial + light industrial. If we use NREL's 200 kW commercial average, install count drops 4x but the scan volume and success fee math stay intact because they're both volume-denominated in GW."

Q5 — "$1.30/W system cost vs NREL $1.78/W?"

"Downward adjustment reflects installer channel efficiency we expect to capture through SolNest standardization. Conservative for our favor — investor upside if correct, no downside if incorrect."
