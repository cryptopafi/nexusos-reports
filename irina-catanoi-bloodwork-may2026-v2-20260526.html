What The Numbers Mean

38 things were measured. 27 are perfect. 11 need attention. Here are the 11 in plain words.

Main flag

Thyroid antibodies are high

TPO Ab = 32 (should be under 9). This is the body's immune system mistakenly identifying the thyroid gland as a threat and producing antibodies against it. The thyroid is still making hormones fine — but the attack has begun. If left alone, over years it slowly destroys thyroid tissue. Stopping it now is the whole game.

Think of it like termites in a house. The walls are still standing, the wood looks fine — but the termites are already chewing. You don't wait until the wall collapses; you act when you spot the first sawdust.

Thyroid hormones are sitting low (TSH 2.21 · Free T4 1.0 · Free T3 2.9)

These three are all "in the lab range" but at the wrong end of it. Her body is asking the thyroid to work harder (TSH is creeping up), and the active form of thyroid hormone (Free T3) is at the bottom of normal. Translation: she's likely a little fatigued, a little colder than she should be, hair maybe shedding more than usual.

Iron stores are low (Ferritin 29)

Ferritin is how much iron your body has stored away for later. Hers is 29 — the lab calls that normal, but for a cycling woman the target is 70–100. Below 50 you start feeling it: fatigue, brain fog, hair shedding, cold hands.

It's a fuel tank that's only 25% full. Car still runs, but you're nervous on every hill.

The hidden problem: iron is also the cofactor the body needs to convert the inactive form of thyroid hormone (T4) into the active form (T3). So low iron is silently making her thyroid problem worse.

Female hormones are softer than ideal

Free testosterone = 1.2 (should be 2.0–3.5). Estradiol = 48 (low for the cycle phase she was in). Progesterone = 4.1 (low). DHEA-S = 102 (middle of normal, but should be 150+). IGF-1 = 151 (a recovery/repair hormone, on the low side).

All five together = the body's "vitality factory" is running slower than it should. Not menopause — just the very first dimming of the lights. Now is the right time to intervene; in 5 years it's harder.

NEW finding

The "stress drain" pattern

When you look at all five hormones above together, they tell a story: chronic stress is siphoning her body's hormone-building supplies into making cortisol (the stress hormone), leaving nothing left over to make the sex hormones.

Picture a single bag of flour. You can make bread or cake or pasta from it. When stress is high, the body bakes nothing but emergency bread (cortisol) all day long. The cake (estrogen) and pasta (testosterone) just don't get made.

This is why we add pregnenolone — it's the raw flour that refills the supply.

Insulin is creeping up (6.6) + triglycerides on the high side (128)

Her blood sugar is perfect, but the amount of insulin needed to keep it that way is starting to rise. Plus her blood fats (triglycerides) are higher than ideal. Both point at one thing: too many refined carbs in the diet.

It's like the body is shouting louder and louder to get the message through. Right now it's still working — but it's working harder than it should.

Homocysteine is borderline (8.4) + B12 not great (620)

Homocysteine is a chemical the body normally clears with B-vitamins. Hers isn't clearing well, which means she's not "methylating" properly — that's the process by which the body activates folate and B12 into usable forms.

Why it matters: methylation is the same process the thyroid needs to function and the same one that protects blood vessels. Fixing it helps both her thyroid problem and her long-term heart health. About 40% of people have a gene (MTHFR) that makes them bad at this — the fix is taking the already-activated forms of the vitamins.

Magnesium is on the low end (2.1)

Lab calls it normal, target is 2.2+. Magnesium runs over 300 enzymes in the body — including ones for sleep, thyroid, and insulin. Cheap to fix, helps with many things at once.

NEW finding

The hidden link: her thyroid problem is making her insulin problem

Here's something not obvious from the numbers alone. The same inflammation that's attacking her thyroid is also the reason her insulin is creeping up. They look like two separate issues — they're actually one.

Imagine the body's cells have ears. Insulin shouts "let sugar in!" — and inflammation is like loud static noise that makes the cells go deaf. Fix the static (the autoimmune inflammation), and the cells can hear insulin again.

This is why we use KPV: it cuts the static at its source. It's a two-for-one — calms the thyroid attack and brings the insulin back into line.

Phase 1 — Daily Supplements (Pills)

These give the body the building blocks it's currently missing. They make the peptides above work better — without them, you're trying to build a house with no bricks.

Iron (bisglycinate form)

25–30 mg, every other morning

Her ferritin is 29, target is 70–100. Iron is also the helper her body needs to turn inactive thyroid hormone (T4) into active thyroid hormone (T3). So we kill two birds: more energy and better thyroid function.

Take with vitamin C on an empty stomach. Every-other-day works better than daily (the body absorbs more this way).

Selenium

200 mcg, morning, with food

The only nutrient with hard scientific evidence that it lowers thyroid antibodies. Multiple RCTs (randomized controlled trials) show TPO antibody numbers drop after 3 months of selenium.

Don't exceed 400 mcg/day — too much selenium is its own problem.

Magnesium (glycinate form)

300–400 mg, at bedtime

Helps sleep deeply, calms the nervous system, supports thyroid function and insulin sensitivity. Glycinate is the form that doesn't upset the stomach.

Methylated B-complex

1 capsule, morning

Her homocysteine is 8.4 (target under 7). This vitamin mix gives her B12 and folate in the "pre-activated" form, so her body doesn't have to do the activation step it's bad at. Drops homocysteine, supports thyroid, protects blood vessels long-term.

It's like buying pre-cooked rice instead of raw rice — saves the body a step it's been struggling with.

Myo-inositol + D-chiro-inositol (40:1 blend)

2,000 mg + 50 mg, twice a day

Two B-vitamin-like compounds with RCT evidence in Hashimoto's: they lower TSH and antibodies. Also reduces insulin resistance — so once again, two-for-one.

Zinc (picolinate form)

25 mg, evening, with food

Third member of the "thyroid trio" — iron + selenium + zinc are all needed for the body to make and activate thyroid hormone. Also supports the immune-peacekeeper cells we're trying to grow.

Vitamin D3 + K2

2,000 IU + 100 mcg, morning with fat

Food rules (non-negotiable)
  • 🚫 No gluten for 90 days. Gluten proteins look like thyroid proteins to a confused immune system — it's a major trigger. Strict, not "mostly." Then we retest antibodies.

  • 🥚 More protein — about 1.6 grams per kilo of bodyweight per day. Muscle needs it, and thyroid hormone is literally made from a protein building block (tyrosine).
