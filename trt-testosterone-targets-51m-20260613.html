SubQ vs IM: Observational community data suggests SubQ administration reduces Hct elevation by ~41% compared to IM (SCOUT-SOCIAL-TRT — observational only, not RCT). If future Hct rise occurs, route conversion to SubQ is an evidence-consistent intervention ahead of dose reduction.

Phlebotomy concern: Repeated phlebotomy depletes iron stores → compensatory erythropoietic drive → perpetuates the Hct elevation, while creating iron-deficiency symptoms.

05 Free Testosterone vs Total Testosterone

Free T (~2% of total T) is the fraction available for receptor binding. Total T includes SHBG (Sex Hormone-Binding Globulin)-bound T (biologically inactive) and albumin-bound T (weakly bioavailable).

On TRT, SHBG is typically suppressed, which increases the free fraction percentage. A patient on TRT with a given total T will have a higher free T percentage than a eugonadal man with the same total T — making total T alone potentially misleading as a monitoring endpoint.

Assay note: Two methods exist: (1) equilibrium dialysis (reference standard) and (2) direct immunoassay (LabCorp direct; widely used, has positive bias). Age-stratified reference ranges should be applied from the same assay platform (7.2–24.0 pg/mL for ages 50–59 by LabCorp direct assay).

06 The Population Reference Range Problem

Reference ranges are derived by sampling healthy adult men across age bands and defining the 2.5th–97.5th percentile as "normal." The key critique: the "healthy" reference population includes men with metabolic syndrome, obesity, T2DM (Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus), and chronic illness — conditions that suppress T.

Bachmeyer corpus (patient note quality): "Current reference ranges are artificially lowered based on a deficient population — historically 750–1,200 ng/dL was the normal range."

Approximately 20% of inter-individual T variation is heritable (Zhai 2011). A genetically high-set-point individual may be functionally hypogonadal at 500 ng/dL while a low-set-point individual is replete at 350 ng/dL.

Micro-Dosing Rationale

The 50 mg q2d or 100 mg q3.5d schedules flatten the TC concentration curve, reducing peak 5-alpha reductase (5αR) saturation and downstream DHT/E2 conversion spikes. This is mechanistically plausible but not directly validated in head-to-head RCTs for DHT outcomes.
