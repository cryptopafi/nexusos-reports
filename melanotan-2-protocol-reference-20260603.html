Every dose below is tagged by evidence quality. Most circulating MT-2 numbers are community-derived, NOT from controlled trials.
Read first — evidence reality: The entire formal human trial database for MT-2 is ~3 small early-phase studies (n=3, n=10, n=10) from 1996–2000. No RCT has established a safe tanning dose; no peer-reviewed safety data beyond 12 weeks exists. The loading/maintenance protocol below emerged from self-experimentation communities, refined by trial-and-error — not validated research. MT-2 is not approved anywhere in the world for any use.

Bedtime = sleep through the nausea/flushing peak (~1–2 h post-injection). UV: MT-2 primes melanocytes but needs ~15–30 min UV 2–3×/wk to actually tan — inject at night, get sun next day. SC (subcutaneous) into abdominal fat, rotate sites.

⚠️ The number the community quietly lowered

The only peer-reviewed human doses were 0.025 mg/kg SC (Dorr 1996; Wessells 1998) — ≈ 1,750 µg for a 70 kg person, ~7–12× higher than community starting doses. The community drifted DOWN to 150–250 µg specifically because the clinical doses caused severe nausea, somnolence and hypertension. So "more" is not better — the low community start is a damage-control adaptation, not an efficacy choice.

Safety — common & serious

Common (dose-dependent, transient) peer-reviewed

Nausea (universal in trials), facial flushing, spontaneous erections (8/10 in ED trial), appetite suppression, somnolence, yawning-stretching complex, darkening of moles/freckles/scars.

Serious adverse events (case reports) clinical:
  • Rhabdomyolysis + AKI — 39M injected 6 mg (grey-market), CK 1,760→17,773 IU/L, 3 days ICU (Nelson 2012, PMID 23121206).
  • Renal infarction — 27 mg over 6 months, ~50% kidney infarcted, new hypertension (Peters 2020).
  • Ischemic priapism requiring surgical decompression, permanent ED in cases (BMJ Case Reports).
  • Hypertensive crises — MC4R-mediated vasopressor effect; sympathomimetic toxicity at supra-dose.

🔴 Melanoma / dermatology — the critical risk

Multiple peer-reviewed case reports describe melanoma or eruptive dysplastic nevi during/shortly after MT-2 use. Causality is not proven in controlled studies, but biological plausibility is strong and the case evidence is consistent:

  • Melanoma after 3–4 wk MT-2 + sunbed, 20F (Hjuler & Lorentzen, Dermatology 2014, PMID 24355990).
  • >100 new nevi / severe dysplasia after 4 wk, 25M (Actas Dermo-Sifiliográficas 2012).
  • Mole→0.3 mm melanoma 3 months after use, 42F (Paurobally).
  • pT4 oral mucosal melanoma after MT-2 nasal spray, 22F (Alsabbagh 2025).
  • Review: ≥4 melanomas-from-moles documented (Habbema, IJD 2017).
Recommended monitoring (dermatologist-authored): (1) baseline full-body skin exam + dermoscopy + photograph all moles BEFORE first use; (2) stop immediately if any mole changes size/shape/colour/border or bleeds; (3) skin check after each course; (4) ≥annual checks (6-monthly if fair-skinned / many moles / family melanoma history).

Contraindications

Melanoma history (personal/family) · atypical nevi / many moles · very fair skin (MC1R variants) · uncontrolled hypertension / cardiovascular disease · prior priapism or thrombosis · hormone-sensitive cancers · renal disease · pregnancy/breastfeeding (zero repro data) · neurological conditions (PRES/seizures reported) · WADA-tested athletes (MT-2 banned, §S2). clinical peer-reviewed

Regulatory & purity

MT-2 is NOT approved anywhere (FDA/EMA/MHRA/TGA/HPRA warnings issued). It is a grey-market research chemical. Distinct from afamelanotide (Scenesse / "MT-1"), which IS FDA/EMA-approved — but only as an implant for erythropoietic protoporphyria (a rare photosensitivity disease), is MC1R-selective, and is a different molecule. That approval does NOT legitimise MT-2.

Purity risk: independent assay (Breindahl 2015) found grey-market "10 mg" vials varied significantly in actual content — no guarantee of dose, purity or sterility. The 6 mg overdose rhabdomyolysis case was enabled by exactly this concentration uncertainty.
