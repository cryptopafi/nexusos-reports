Negotiation Card — Top Pick (Sapphire 6BR Frond L)

Negotiation reality (H10 amplified): On a SPA assignment, seller has THREE structural constraints:

  1. Nakheel approval — assignment fee ~AED 50K + 30-day window. Cannot bypass.
  2. Installment schedule frozen — buyer steps into existing payment plan as-is.
  3. Original SPA price fixed — only the premium-on-top is negotiable.

If seller paid 20% (AED 3.9M) + wants AED 3M premium → buyer negotiates on the AED 3M only. Realistic concession band = AED 0.5M–1.0M off premium.

  • Opening offer: AED 21.0M (premium ~AED 1.5M, half of ask) — anchor on Frond L Phase-1 handover risk + assignment fee + 4% DLD on buyer.
  • Walk-away: AED 21.5M.
  • Concessions to extract instead of price: seller pays AED 50K assignment fee; seller absorbs 2% broker commission (AED 430K); long-shot: seller covers DLD 4% (AED 800K+).
  • Likely outcome: AED 21.8M with seller covering assignment fee + half broker commission. Effective discount ~AED 700K (3.1% off ask).

Critical caveat: Negotiation power on PJA SPAs is structurally weaker than on completed villas. No DOM clock pressure on seller — they're not paying mortgage interest, only pre-budgeted installments. They can park the listing for months. Capital-deploying buyers are at a structural disadvantage.
