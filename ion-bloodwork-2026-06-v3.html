Plain-Words Layer — what the red items mean

🔴 Insulin resistance (HOMA-IR 16, insulin 56.6, glucose 116)
💬 In plain words: Your body still keeps blood sugar near-normal, but only because the pancreas pumps out ~6× more insulin than it should to get there. Like flooring the gas pedal just to drive at normal speed — the engine is straining and will eventually tire out. The single most important thing to fix, and very reversible with diet, movement, weight loss, and the metabolic peptides below.
🔴 Gamma-GT 356 (6.5× upper limit), normal AST/ALT
💬 In plain words: GGT is a liver "stress" enzyme. Very high while the other two liver enzymes are normal usually points to fatty liver and/or alcohol/oxidative load, not active damage yet. Needs a liver ultrasound and 90 days off alcohol. Typically falls fast once insulin resistance and visceral fat come down.
🔴 Eosinophils 10.8% (high)
💬 In plain words: Immune cells that rise with allergies or a parasite. Before any immune-affecting peptide, a simple stool test + IgE blood test should rule out a parasite — cheap, important, easy.
🔴 Low free testosterone (14) + low estradiol (<10)
💬 In plain words: The "active" form of testosterone your tissues can use is slightly low, and so is estrogen. Total testosterone is okay, so this is mostly downstream of the insulin/metabolic problem, not primary testicle failure. As metabolic health improves, these usually recover on their own — so we fix the root first rather than jumping to testosterone injections.
🟡 MCV 98 (just over) & kidney filtration 86 (mildly under 90)
💬 In plain words: Both only a hair outside the line and not alarming on their own. Worth a recheck plus B12/folate (for the slightly large red cells) and hydration/kidney over time.

Bachmeyer Protocol — Peptides: Full Dosing

Cross-referenced against Bachmeyer NLM (cb9cf1fd + 1eef6220 + Peptide Dosage d9213daf) and the Bachmeyer LLM-Wiki. All doses are Bachmeyer-cited or standard-range — never invented; exact dose is physician-set, start low. All injectables are subcutaneous (SubQ) unless noted. Nothing starts before the pre-peptide workup clears.

Tier 1 — Metabolic core

1. Retatrutide — triple GLP-1/GIP/glucagon agonist · the engine

  • Dose/titration: start 0.5 mg SubQ once weekly, hold 2 weeks, then +0.5 mg every 4 weeks (slow — fatty liver makes faster steps risk nausea/dehydration/AKI), climbing toward 2 mg/week (max ~4 mg/week if needed and tolerated). Once weekly — NOT daily / not micro-dosed.
  • Reconstitution: 10 mg vial + 2 mL bacteriostatic water → 10 units (0.1 mL) = 0.5 mg.
  • Bachmeyer tracks: HbA1c (→~5.2%), fasting insulin + HOMA-IR ↓, ALT/AST normalize (liver fat cleared), triglycerides↓/HDL↑, hs-CRP↓. Baseline lipase/amylase before the Week-5 step (pancreatitis is the titration risk).
💬 In plain words: The single most important agent — it hits all three problems at once (blood sugar, insulin, and fatty liver), because its glucagon action makes the liver burn its own stored fat. Go up slowly. One injection a week, never daily. Bachmeyer prefers it over Ozempic/Mounjaro here (those use fewer pathways and ~70% of weight comes back after stopping).

2. Tesamorelin — ADDED · GHRH analogue · visceral/internal-fat killer

  • Dose: 1–2 mg SubQ nightly, 30–60 min before bed, empty stomach (aligns with the natural deep-sleep GH pulse).
  • Cycle: 5 days on / 2 days off; therapeutic blocks of 12–16 weeks then a break; minimum 6–9 months for a meaningful IGF-1/visceral-fat effect.
  • Bachmeyer tracks: IGF-1 — meaningful only from ~Week 12, not week 1. IGF-1 129 (mid-normal) is the green light: headroom to support Tesamorelin while staying in the normal band. Watch IGF-1 doesn't over-shoot (over-pushing can worsen insulin resistance — keep in range, re-check Week 12).
  • Synergy: stacks with Retatrutide for visceral fat (different mechanisms, same target). Do NOT mix with Ipamorelin in the same protocol (another reason Ipamorelin is held).
💬 In plain words: The one specifically for deep "internal"/belly fat around the organs — it nudges your own pituitary to release growth hormone in natural night-time pulses, which melts visceral fat (about a 25% reduction in studies). Take it at night on an empty stomach. Expect a little water-weight puffiness in week 1 that passes. Your IGF-1 (129) is healthy enough to fuel this without the other GH peptides.

3. MOTS-c — mitochondrial peptide

  • Dose: 5–10 mg SubQ, 2–3×/week (or ~5 mg every other day). Can run indefinitely. (Common error is dosing this in micrograms — it is milligrams.)
  • Bachmeyer tracks: fasting insulin + HOMA-IR ↓.
💬 In plain words: A tune-up for the cell's "batteries" so muscles burn sugar properly — it directly improves insulin sensitivity and works best alongside walking/training.

4. 5-Amino-1MQ — ADDED · NNMT inhibitor · stored-fat unlock

  • Route: SubQ ONLY — Bachmeyer says the oral form is ineffective.
  • Dose: start conservative ~10–20 mg/day into belly fat in the morning / pre-workout, titrate up over cycles toward 50 mg/day (Bachmeyer's stated range 50–100 mg/day; start low given the metabolic load).
  • Cycle: must be cycled — 6 weeks on / 2 off (or NLM's 10-on/10-off) due to fast receptor attenuation.
💬 In plain words: Helps "unlock" fat cells so the stored fat can actually be burned — it pairs with Retatrutide and Tesamorelin so the fat you mobilize gets used. Inject into belly fat in the morning. Must be cycled on and off or it stops working.

5. BPC-157 — repair / anti-inflammatory

  • Dose: 250 mcg–1 mg/day SubQ; cycle 5 on / 2 off advisable (rather than open-ended).
  • Bachmeyer tracks: hs-CRP + symptoms.
💬 In plain words: A gut- and tissue-healing peptide that calms inflammation — used here to support the gut–liver axis while everything else works.
Tier 2 — Adjuncts (after Tier 1 tolerated + workup clear)

6. Cardarine (GW-501516) — PPAR-δ agonist (not a peptide)

  • Dose/route (Bachmeyer): SubQ 30 min before workouts (SubQ preferred over the common 20 mg/day oral — bypasses the liver's first-pass, which matters with this GGT/fatty-liver picture); cycled 30 days on / 30 days off (non-negotiable cycling).
  • Bachmeyer tracks: HDL↑, triglycerides↓, LDL particles↓.
💬 In plain words: Flips the body to burn fat for fuel and improves cholesterol — a direct help for the liver and lipids. Always cycle it a month on, a month off.

7. KPV — anti-inflammatory tripeptide

  • Dose: 300–400 mcg/day SubQ (acute gut flare: 500 mcg single).
  • Gate: only after the eosinophilia (parasite/IgE) workup is clean — it touches the immune system.
  • Bachmeyer tracks: hs-CRP.
💬 In plain words: A gentle inflammation-calmer for the elevated CRP — but we wait until the eosinophil/parasite test is clear before using it.

Hormone Restoration — natural-first (after metabolic root is addressed)

8. Kisspeptin-10 — preferred

  • Dose: 100–500 mcg SubQ, 2–3×/week, max cycle 8–10 weeks (pulsatile).
💬 In plain words: The "ignition key" that tells the brain to restart your own testosterone — it preserves natural function instead of replacing it. Bachmeyer's first choice, well ahead of testosterone injections.

9. Enclomiphene — alternative / if free-T stays low

  • Dose: oral (Bachmeyer); standard practitioner dose ~12.5 mg/day or every other day — exact cycle physician-set. Reserve if free T is still low after the metabolic fix.
💬 In plain words: A pill that nudges the pituitary to make more of its own testosterone signal — a good fit for this "secondary" low-T. Held in reserve in case the metabolic fix alone doesn't lift free testosterone.
🚫 No first-line TRT (testosterone injections shut down your own production) · 🚫 no aromatase inhibitors (estradiol is already low). Both are Bachmeyer positions.

⏸️ Held — do NOT start yet

Ipamorelin + CJC-1295 — GH secretagogue stack, deferred

💬 In plain words: We are not starting these now. Tesamorelin already covers the growth-hormone / visceral-fat job, and IGF-1 (129) doesn't need a bigger push. Also, Ipamorelin must never be run in the same protocol as Tesamorelin. Revisit later only if needed.
(Reference doses for the future: CJC-1295 no-DAC 100–300 mcg + Ipamorelin 100–200 mcg nightly, 5 on/2 off — held.)

  • ApoB + Lp(a) (true cardiovascular risk beyond LDL)
  • Homocysteine, B12, folate, vitamin D (methylation + deficiencies)
  • 90-day alcohol abstinence, then re-draw GGT
