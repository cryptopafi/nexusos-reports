Section 3 — System-level Bachmeyer interpretation

Primary problem: Severe insulin resistance + suspected NAFLD as one circuit, not two diseases

In the Bachmeyer framing (health_bachmeyer corpus repeatedly), NAFLD is not a separate hepatic disease — it is the inevitable downstream consequence of chronic hyperinsulinemia driving inappropriate hepatic de novo lipogenesis (transcript [44:02]): “NAFLD develops because of hyperinsulinemia, chronic high insulin, driving inappropriate hepatic lipogenesis. When insulin levels are normalized and hepatic insulin sensitivity comes back online, hepatic lipid accumulation stops and goes the other direction.” So treating Borzea Ioan’s HOMA-IR 16 and GGT 356 as two independent problems would be a category error. They are one circuit: visceral adiposity → hyperinsulinemia → hepatic lipotoxicity → fibrogenic activation → GGT elevation.

Mechanistic loop (Bachmeyer-cited)

  1. Lipotoxicity at the cellular level: intramuscular and intrahepatic triglycerides + diacylglycerols block the insulin receptor signaling cascade by activating PKC → phosphorylates IRS1 at inhibitory residues (health_bachmeyer [18:01–18:10]: “these lipids impair insulin signaling. They literally block the insulin receptor signaling cascade”).
  2. Compensatory hyperinsulinemia: the pancreas screams louder (insulin 56.6) at deaf cells. HOMA-B 384% = the scream is at maximum volume.
  3. Hepatic insulin resistance: the liver becomes insulin-resistant too, fails to clear circulating insulin properly, perpetuates hyperinsulinemia.
  4. Inflammatory amplification: TNF-α / IL-6 / IL-1β (driven by visceral adipose) activate serine kinases that further phosphorylate IRS1 → vicious cycle (health_bachmeyer [25:16–25:36]: “TNF alpha, IL6 … activate serine kinases that phosphorylate insulin receptor substrate 1, IRS1, at the wrong spot, prevents proper glucose uptake into the cell. So you become insulin deaf, insulin resistant.”).
  5. Pancreatic exhaustion ahead: continued overdrive will eventually produce beta-cell dysfunction (the second component of type 2 diabetes per Bachmeyer’s framework). Borzea is currently still in compensated-prediabetic phase; this window of reversibility is the clinical opportunity.

Secondary: Mitochondrial dysfunction as the upstream lever

Bachmeyer treats mitochondrial dysfunction as the root cause both of NAFLD and insulin resistance (health_bachmeyer [16:53–17:13]: “You’ve got fatty liver. The root cause is mitochondrial dysfunction leading to fatty acid accumulation and lipid peroxidation … You’ve got diabetes. The root cause is mitochondrial dysfunction in pancreatic beta cells and muscle and liver insulin resistance”). Pharmacological GLP-1 agonism alone (semaglutide, tirzepatide) does not restore mitochondrial function, which Bachmeyer cites as the explanation for the catastrophic ~70% weight-regain post-discontinuation in published trials (SUSTAIN, SURMOUNT). This drives his Tier-1 stack toward mitochondrial-rebuilding peptides alongside the metabolic-control peptide.

Tertiary: Hormonal axis softening (HPG)

The estradiol <10 + low-functional LH + DHEAS 109 + SHBG 51.7 + prolactin 15.5 cluster suggests HPG-axis suppression driven by the same metabolic dysfunction — chronic hyperinsulinemia and inflammation impair hypothalamic kisspeptin signaling, which then under-drives the entire male hormonal cascade. Treating only the hormones (testosterone replacement) without addressing insulin resistance produces poor outcomes. Treating the metabolic axis first often restores hormones autonomously.

Quaternary: Eosinophilia of unknown cause

Eosinophils 10.8% / 0.81 absolute is the open question on the panel. Most likely causes in this region (Eastern Europe, Romania): intestinal parasitosis (Strongyloides, Giardia, Ascaris) > atopic disease > drug reaction > rarer (eosinophilic gastroenteritis, hypereosinophilic syndrome). Must be resolved before initiating BPC-157 or KPV — the immune-modulation profile is parasite-favorable in the unresolved case and could mask diagnosis.

MCV 98 + GGT 356 — the alcohol question

The combination needs an honest patient conversation. The biochemical signature is non-specific (could be either alcohol or pure metabolic NAFLD), but the probability weighting favors at least some alcohol contribution when both markers are out together. If alcohol is in fact a driver, the protocol must include alcohol cessation as a precondition — peptides alone will not overcome ongoing hepatotoxicity.

  • Documented effects in profile-matched humans:
    • HOMA-IR improvement 68% (NEJM 2023) (health_bachmeyer [19:56])
    • Hepatic fat content reduction 51% (JAMA 2023, NAFLD patients) (health_bachmeyer [15:08–15:12])

    • Pilot study: complete NASH resolution in 6/8 patients in 16 weeks (health_bachmeyer [30:11–30:21])
  • Dose / route / cycle (Bachmeyer-cited starting range): 1–2 mg SC weekly, titrated very slowly from 0.5 mg; reassess at week 8 and week 16. Maximum tolerated dose individual.
