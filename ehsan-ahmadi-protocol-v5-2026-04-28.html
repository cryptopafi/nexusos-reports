4.4 — Lipid & Statin Position

Bachmeyer position: vehemently against statins. They poison HMG-CoA (3-hydroxy-3-methyl-glutaryl coenzyme A) reductase, deplete CoQ10 (coenzyme Q10), damage mitochondria, impair cognition, and crash hormone production. He considers total cholesterol 275–300 mg/dL healthy as a substrate for brain, cell membranes, and steroid hormones. The danger is oxidised cholesterol in inflamed endothelium, not the absolute LDL number.

Bachmeyer's lipid protocol for Ehsan's pattern (high LDL, excellent TG/HDL):

  • Retatrutide + Cardarine (already active) → −21% LDL, hepatic VLDL clearance, fatty-acid oxidation.
  • BPC-157 + KPV (already active) → endothelial repair + anti-inflammatory, blocking LDL oxidation.
  • No additional Bachmeyer protocol exists for isolated high-LDL/excellent-TG-HDL pattern beyond what is already active.

Note for physician: confirmed calcific LAD plaque is a Class I AHA/ESC (American Heart Association / European Society of Cardiology) indication for statin therapy. This is a clinician decision in dialogue with Ehsan. If statin is initiated despite Bachmeyer's position, prefer rosuvastatin over atorvastatin (better hepatic profile given liver steatosis), add Ubiquinol 200 mg/day to offset CoQ10 depletion, and recheck ALT/AST at 6 weeks.

4.5 — E2 Normalisation (E2 46.95 HIGH)

Bachmeyer position: NO aromatase inhibitors (anastrozole, exemestane). They only block the enzyme; once stopped, aromatase rebounds violently above baseline.
  • DIM (diindolylmethane) 300 mg/day — enhances Phase 2 liver conjugation, converts estrogen to water-soluble form for urinary excretion. ~30% reduction in circulating estrogen.
  • Retatrutide-driven adipose loss — eliminates aromatase at the source. Target body fat <15%, ideally 10%.
  • Enclomiphene (variant per original notebook) — blocks estrogen receptors at pituitary/hypothalamus to force upstream correction. Reserve if E2 remains high after 8 weeks DIM + Retatrutide.

4.6 — Testosterone Strategy (Free T 13 low-normal, Total T 472)

Bachmeyer prefers natural restoration over exogenous TRT (testosterone replacement therapy) because TRT shuts down endogenous production.

  • Kisspeptin (preferred): the master switch — stimulates GnRH (gonadotropin-releasing hormone) neurons → LH/FSH → natural testosterone, preserving testicular function.
  • Enclomiphene: appropriate for secondary hypogonadism (likely given low IGF-1 + HPA suppression upstream).
  • If TRT chosen: IM (intramuscular) micro-dose — 50 mg every 2nd day OR 100 mg every 3.5 days. Cypionate/Enanthate 100–150 mg/wk for primary hypogonadism only. Never transdermal creams/gels — localised DHT (dihydrotestosterone) spike drives prostate growth.

Free T should rise once E2 drops with DIM + Retatrutide. Recheck at 8 weeks before any T intervention.

4.11 — GHK-Cu Protocol (currently 2 mg/day)

  • Dose: 2 mg/day SubQ confirmed (Extended notebook). Original notebook range 300 mcg–2–3 mg/day.
  • Timing: morning, fasted (or with food if GI sensitivity). Space hours from growth-factor peptides.
  • HARD rule: NEVER mix in syringe with other peptides. Reactive copper ion immediately destroys co-injected peptides. Avoid all blend products (KLOW/GLOW etc).
  • Cycling: mandatory to prevent copper toxicity. Standard practice 8 weeks on / 2–4 weeks off.

4.12 — Cardarine (currently 20 mg/day oral)

  • Bachmeyer position: highly recommended. PPAR-delta (peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor delta) agonist; forces fat oxidation, mitochondrial biogenesis, raises HDL, lowers TG, clears atherogenic LDL particles.
  • Bachmeyer protocol: SubQ injection 30 minutes before workouts; cycle 30 days on / 30 days off.
  • Cancer myth (debunked by Bachmeyer): rodent carcinogenicity used 100–320× human therapeutic dose via continuous implant; human safety data shows zero carcinogenic signal.
  • Route: Ehsan currently oral 20 mg/day. Dose within range, but oral adds first-pass hepatic load on top of confirmed liver steatosis. Switching to SubQ aligns with Bachmeyer protocol and reduces hepatic burden.
