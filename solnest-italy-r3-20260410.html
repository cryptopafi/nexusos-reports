What Law 193/2024 Actually Changed

Law 193/2024 Art 28 (Legge Annuale Concorrenza 2024, GU 17-12-2024) modified Art 25 by:

Change 1 — NEW lettera a-bis)

Startup Innovativa must qualify as an EU SME (Recommendation 2003/361/EC): ≤250 employees, ≤€50M revenue, ≤€43M balance sheet.

Change 2 — MODIFIED lettera f)

Cannot have agency or consulting as primary activity.

Change 3 — Investor terminology

Replaces "investitori professionali" with "investitori qualificati" in Art 29 investor tax credit provisions. This affects who gets tax credits when investing, not who can own the startup.

PRIMARY-SOURCE DOCUMENTATION

MIMIT Circular 29 July 2025 is the most authoritative 2026 source — explicitly silent on foreign ownership. The operative SME test is size-based, not ownership-nationality-based. Full text retrieved from normattiva.it.

The Real Risk: SME Consolidation

Per EU Recommendation 2003/361/EC (now embedded in Italian Startup law via Law 193/2024):

  • • Delaware C-Corp owns 100% of Italian SRL → full consolidation applies
  • • Combined group must qualify as SME: ≤250 employees, ≤€50M revenue
  • • For SolNest current stage: Delaware parent is a micro-startup → passes comfortably
  • • Future risk: As Delaware grows, Italian SRL may lose Startup Innovativa status at scale
FUTURE MONITORING

Delaware parent growing past SME limits (>250 employees or >€50M revenue) will disqualify the Italian SRL from Startup Innovativa status. This is a monitoring trigger, not a current blocker.

Six Questions — Compressed Answers

Q1 — Art 25 current text

No natural-person majority requirement. lettera a) = 60-month age limit only. Full Art 25 comma 2 text at normattiva.it: zero ownership nationality criteria. Amendments: DL 76/2013 (extended time), L. 193/2024 (added SME + consulting ban).

Q2 — Law 193/2024 changes

Added SME requirement (size-based, not nationality-based). Modified investor tax-credit terminology. Effective 18-12-2024. No foreign-parent language.

Q3 — Foreign-owned SRL, 2026 reality

MIMIT Circular 29 July 2025 confirms no foreign-ownership prohibition. Registration via autocertificazione. No case law rejecting a foreign-owned SRL from the startup register. Only exclusion: "grandi imprese" control (via SME test).

Q4 — Workaround (Pafi personally owns Italian SRL)

Viable. Transfer pricing required under Art 110 TUIR. Annual documentation cost: €5K-€15K. Risk of abuso del diritto (Art 10-bis L. 212/2000) if structure lacks economic substance. Recommended: APA with Agenzia delle Entrate or a pre-filing interpello.

Q5 — Smart&Start 2026

OPEN (sportello permanente). €100K-€1.5M at 0% rate. 30% non-repayable grant for Southern Italy (Calabria, Puglia, Sicilia, Basilicata, Campania, Molise, Abruzzo, Sardegna) confirmed. No foreign-parent ownership bar in program rules. Must be registered Startup Innovativa.

Q6 — Final verdict

YES conditional. Italy stays on the board. See Scenarios A & B below.
