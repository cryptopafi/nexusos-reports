4. Delivery / Mortgage / Geopolitical Findings

PJA Delivery Status (March 2026)
MODERATE RISK
Active construction confirmed: Beach + Coral Collection. AED 750M+ infrastructure contracts active, completion targeted Q4 2026. AED 5B villa contracts for fronds 1–6 (Oct 2024) targeted late 2026.

Official handover: November 2028. "2026 handover" claims in secondary listings are infrastructure milestones, not villa delivery. Cobalt frond: Q3–Q4 2029 realistic estimate.

Investment implication: If PJA slips to H1 2029, effective seasoned hold before 2030 exit = 12–18 months. Plan for potential 2031 exit window.
Mortgage Reality (Non-Resident IO)
4.5% IO = FAVORABLE END
CBUAE hard cap: 50% LTV for non-residents. ADCB + Emirates NBD both confirm non-resident mortgages at this LTV.

IO at 4.5%: Available but aspirational. Variable EIBOR + 1.75–2.5% implies ~3.75–4.5% effective. Use 5.0–5.25% as base-case stress test.

Critical: Off-plan assets (Oasis, PJA, EDITION) = NO mortgage during construction. Full cash or developer payment plan until handover. Mortgage only at completion. 100% equity deployed for 2–3 years before leverage is possible.
Russian Capital Flow
Active through 2025, decelerating. Palm AED 30M+ luxury purchases still Russia-driven. Risk: ceasefire scenario could reduce flow. Structural until 2027; diminishing 2028–2030.
India HNWI
9,800 HNWIs relocating to UAE in 2025 (Henley). India avg budget USD 44.6M (KF Wealth 2025). Structural, compounding trend — unlikely to reverse. Provides durable 2030 exit bid.
Oversupply Risk
160K+ unit pipeline. 85% apartments. Ultra-prime villa supply physically constrained. Emaar Oasis most exposed (7,000+ units). PJA, Palm, Al Barari insulated. US recession = net neutral to mild positive for Dubai ultra-prime (safe-haven inflows).

5. Quality Flags

RED — No DLD Closes: All Emaar Oasis sub-communities. Entirely off-plan. All pricing is developer list or secondary ask. Base rates carry ±20% uncertainty.
RED — No DLD Closes: PJA Beach/Coral/Cobalt. Same as Oasis. Off-plan with no secondary close history. Resale AED/sqft may not hold at scale delivery.
AMBER — Thin secondary: Al Barari Nest & Lunaria. <20 transactions/year community-wide. Broker narrative (ask prices) may be 15–25% above actual close prices. DLD data sparse by design (private community).
AMBER — Single outlier: Palm Jumeirah Signature. AED 14,679/sqft (AED 161M sale) is a market-defining outlier, not the median. Use AED 7,500–11,000 psf as realistic range; outlier skews averages significantly upward.
AMBER — Ask vs close gap: Palm Standard Frond. 5–8% below median ask is the close gap per market data. Reduce ask prices by 5–8% for underwriting inputs.
AMBER — IO pricing model input. 4.5% IO is favorable end of market. Base case: 5.0–5.25%. Adjust upward by 50–75bps for stress test in ROE model.
GREEN — Strong data: Knight Frank Prime Index. AED 3,767–3,850 psf Q2–Q3 2025; 500 $10M+ transactions in 2025. Reliable benchmark for Palm Jumeirah standard frond floor.
GREEN — PJA delivery infrastructure confirmed. AED 750M contracts, active construction Q1 2026. 2028 handover base case intact; 2029 slip manageable for 2030 exit with pre-handover mark-to-market option.
GREEN — India HNWI demand structure. USD 44.6M average budget, structural not cyclical flow. Provides durable bid supporting 2030 exit prices.

6. Delta vs Your Model

Where primary 2025 data diverges materially from common assumptions.

Al Barari Nest — HIGHER than general market narrative (+40–70%)
General narrative: AED 2,500–4,000/sqft. 2025 DLD reality: AED 6,316/sqft DLD close (May 2025); active listings at AED 4,118–6,759/sqft. If your model used AED 3,000–4,000/sqft entry, it is materially below current market.
Al Barari Lunaria — Same premium elevation
The Collection (comparable) reached AED 7,518/sqft ask; record close AED 6,316/sqft. If model assumed AED 3,500–5,000/sqft, revise to AED 5,000–7,500/sqft for turnkey alpha.
Emaar Oasis Palmiera — DIFFERENT product tier (suburban lagoon, not waterfront island)
Palmiera sits at only AED 1,350–1,900/sqft. NOT in the same price tier as Palm Jumeirah or Al Barari despite Emaar branding. If model blended "Dubai luxury villa" AED/sqft, Oasis is significantly below that benchmark.
Knight Frank Prime Index AED 3,850 is a 10-community BLEND, not Palm-specific
Palm standard frond actual closes track AED 4,000–5,500/sqft for typical garden homes — materially above the blended index. Signature/Atlantis frond is a separate category entirely (AED 9,000–14,679/sqft).
PJA handover: NOT 2026–2027 — Official delivery is November 2028
Early marketing implied earlier delivery. Infrastructure completes Q4 2026; villa handover is November 2028. Adjust all PJA hold period calculations accordingly. Cobalt frond: 2029 realistic.
Off-plan mortgage = CASH required during construction (no day-1 leverage for Oasis/PJA/EDITION)
A model assuming 50% LTV IO from day 1 on these assets is incorrect. Full cash (or developer payment plan) required until handover. Effective capital commitment is 100% equity during 2–3 year construction phase. Mortgage only available at completion.
KF "3% prime growth" for 2026 is a near-term single-year call, NOT a 4-year CAGR
For 4-year hold ROE modeling, use 6–8% base CAGR (supported by population inflows, constrained supply, HNWI demand). The 3% view reflects 2026-specific moderation post-record 2025, not the secular 2026–2030 trend.

7. ROE Model Inputs (50% LTV / 4.5% IO / 7% entry / 2% exit / 4yr hold)

Key finding: 100% ROE target requires ~12.5% annual CAGR on underlying asset. Achievable only in HIGH scenario for Palm Signature and Al Barari. Base-case (7% CAGR) delivers ~80–90% ROE before exit costs for most sub-markets.

100% ROE math: Total cost drag = 7% entry + 2% exit + 9% IO carry (4.5% x 4yr x 50% LTV) = 18–20%. For 100% net ROE on 50% equity, underlying asset must appreciate ~60% (60% appreciation on 50% LTV = 120% equity return, minus 20% costs = 100% ROE). Requires 12.5% CAGR/yr.
