Technology & project-stage terms

TermStands forWhat it means in plain English
TRLTechnology Readiness LevelEU 1-9 scale measuring how mature a technology is. TRL 1 = basic idea on paper. TRL 9 = proven in commercial use. See scale below.
TRL 1Basic principles observedScientific idea exists, nothing built yet
TRL 2Technology concept formulatedConcept written down, no practical proof
TRL 3Experimental proof of conceptCritical function demonstrated analytically or in lab
TRL 4Technology validated in labComponents validated together in lab environment (e.g. algorithm proven on test data)
TRL 5Technology validated in relevant environmentComponents validated in a setting resembling real conditions
TRL 6Technology demonstrated in relevant environmentPrototype or MVP tested in near-operational setting. EIC Accelerator hard gate. SolNest pre-MVP = TRL 1-3, post-MVP with paying users = TRL 6+.
TRL 7System prototype demonstration in operational environmentFull prototype in actual operational setting
TRL 8System complete and qualifiedFinal product tested and qualified through demonstration
TRL 9Actual system proven in operational environmentCommercial product in real deployment
MVPMinimum Viable ProductFirst working version a real user can actually use. For SolNest, this is the moment the solar marketplace website lets a homeowner book a quote from an installer.
PoCProof of ConceptSmall experiment proving the idea works, usually before MVP
SaaSSoftware as a ServiceSoftware delivered via web subscription (which SolNest is)
R&DResearch and DevelopmentThe qualifying activity for most grants (WBSO, CDTI Neotec, Innosuisse)
SAFESimple Agreement for Future EquityEquity investment instrument (YC-invented) — converts to shares later. Kima Ventures uses these.

EU state-aid framework terms

TermStands forWhat it means in plain English
SMESmall and Medium EnterpriseEU official definition: <250 employees, ≤€50M revenue, ≤€43M balance sheet. Most startup grants require SME status.
Micro enterprise—EU sub-category of SME: <10 employees, ≤€2M revenue. SolNest qualifies as micro in every EU country.
SME consolidation testRecommendation 2003/361/ECRule that decides whether a sub-company is judged alone or together with its parent. Because the Delaware parent has 0 employees and €0 revenue, every SolNest EU sub passes as micro-enterprise on its own.
de minimisLatin "of minimal things"EU rule: small amounts of state aid (up to €300K per company over any 3-year window) are exempt from state-aid review because they're too small to distort markets.
GBERGeneral Block Exemption Regulation (EU Regulation 651/2014)The EU rulebook that pre-approves certain categories of state aid (R&D, startup aid, regional aid) so governments don't have to ask Brussels every time. Higher ceilings than de minimis.
State aid—Any government-granted advantage to a business (grant, loan below market rate, tax break). EU monitors to prevent unfair competition. NL WBSO is explicitly NOT state aid (general tax measure).
GBER Art 22Article 22 "Aid for start-ups"The specific GBER article Austria's AWS Preseed uses. Allows up to €0.4M grant for unlisted SMEs <5 years old.
PNRRPiano Nazionale di Ripresa e Resilienza (Italian National Recovery and Resilience Plan)Italy's version of the EU Recovery Fund implementation. Romania and other EU countries each have their own national recovery plan under the same EU umbrella.

Stackability matrix — what combines with what

Core EU state-aid rules (plain-English)

  • Each EU subsidiary receiving aid is a SEPARATE beneficiary. Different subs getting aid in their respective countries = NOT double-dipping.
  • EU SME consolidation test (Rec. 2003/361/EC) applies per subsidiary. Delaware parent = 0 employees, €0 revenue → each EU sub qualifies independently as micro-enterprise.
  • De minimis ceiling: €300K per entity over 3 rolling fiscal years (cumulated across all de minimis aid).
  • GBER (General Block Exemption Regulation) programs have higher intensity ceilings and can be combined up to eligible cost cap.
  • NL WBSO is classified as a general tax measure, NOT state aid → does NOT count toward de minimis or GBER ceilings. Universal backbone.
