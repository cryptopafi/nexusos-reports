§1 Executive Summary

In 2026, no single agentic harness class dominates enterprise AI across all time horizons. Vendor-managed platforms (Anthropic Agent SDK, OpenAI Agents API, Google Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform, Microsoft Azure AI Foundry) win on time-to-production and capability velocity in the 0–12 month window, but their lock-in trajectories — Anthropic's dynamic enterprise pricing shift in April 2026, demonstrated access revocations, and MCP CVEs on Anthropic's own servers — make them risky as sole long-term bets. Open-source harnesses (LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen/AG2, Agno, Pydantic-AI, Letta) increasingly win on observability, compliance auditability, model portability, and total cost of ownership, especially post-LangGraph v1.0 (October 2025) and the Linux Foundation's absorption of both MCP and A2A under the Agentic AI Foundation. Custom-built internal orchestration — executed by Bloomberg, JPMorgan, Shopify, Stripe — remains the only viable path for Fortune-500 organizations with classified workloads, proprietary AI IP, or sovereign-AI mandates.

The critical strategic insight for 2026: the tool-integration cost differential between vendor-managed and open-source harnesses is collapsing as MCP reaches 10,000 registered servers and 97 million monthly SDK downloads. The decisive selection axis is shifting from "which harness has more built-in tools" to "which harness gives us the compliance auditability and model portability we need for our threat model." A layered architecture — vendor inference APIs at the model layer + OSS orchestration at the control plane + custom compliance enforcement at the governance layer — is the hedge strategy adopted by the most sophisticated enterprise deployers (ServiceNow's simultaneous multi-year deals with both OpenAI and Anthropic is the reference implementation).

The EU AI Act High-Risk compliance window opens August 2026, requiring continuous monitoring, incident reporting, and post-market logging for agentic systems in high-risk sectors. This is an immediate forcing function: organizations in fintech, healthtech, and EU-regulated markets that have not implemented compliant observability infrastructure by Q3 2026 face enforcement exposure. None of the vendor-managed harnesses provide out-of-the-box EU AI Act High-Risk compliance; all require supplementary instrumentation, and open-source harnesses with transparent audit trails (LangSmith + LangGraph) provide the most viable compliance-by-design path.
