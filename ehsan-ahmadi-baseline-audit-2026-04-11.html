Tier-1 Mandatory Tests — BLOCKERS Before Peptide Start

Ordered by safety priority. Ideally batched into one blood draw + one imaging appointment to minimize friction.

  1. hs-CRP — Inflammation biomarker. For a LAD-plaque patient this is the top residual-risk indicator. Per Bachmeyer: "CRP is 2.3× more predictive of cardiovascular death than LDL." Also the baseline that BPC-157 / TB-500 / KPV anti-inflammatory claims are measured against.
  2. apoB (apolipoprotein B) — Better than LDL-C for atherogenic particle count. Current guidelines increasingly use apoB <65 mg/dL as the secondary-prevention target. Drives statin intensity and PCSK9 decision.
  3. Lp(a) — One-time genetic test. ~20% of Europeans carry atherogenic levels independent of LDL. In a premature CAD case (LAD plaque at 42), this is standard-of-care and changes management if elevated (aggressive LDL lowering, aspirin, PCSK9 consideration).
  4. Coronary Artery Calcium Score (Agatston, gated cardiac CT) — Quantifies the "millimetric calcific LAD plaque" descriptor into a number on the 0 → >1000 scale. Score <100 is mild, 100–400 moderate, >400 severe, >1000 extreme. Drives secondary-prevention intensity AND gates angiogenic peptide safety (TB-500 specifically).
  5. IGF-BP3 — Required before Tesamorelin+Ipamorelin. IGF-1/IGFBP-3 ratio is the bioactive readout; IGF-1 alone (measured: 75 LOW) is insufficient for safe dose titration of a GHRH+GHRP stack.
  6. Amylase + Lipase — Required before Retatrutide. GLP-1 class carries a pancreatitis signal; a pre-Rx baseline lets you detect delta if abdominal symptoms develop.
  7. Calcitonin (basal) — Required before Retatrutide. C-cell / MTC baseline gate for GLP-1 class. Thyroid US TIRADS 1 is reassuring, but calcitonin is the biochemical gate.
  8. PSA (total + free, with free/total ratio) — Required before any GHRH+GHRP stack AND for LUTS workup closure. 5-year LUTS at 42M without a PSA is a standing gap.
  9. Full pituitary panel: LH, FSH, Prolactin, FT4, FT3, Cortisol AM, DHEA-S — Required before Tesa+Ipa. Rules out hypopituitarism / secondary hypogonadism / pituitary adenoma as the cause of low IGF-1 + low-normal Free T. Changes interpretation of the whole stack decision.
  10. Urine ACR + Urinalysis with microscopy — Required before Retatrutide (renal baseline) AND closes the LUTS hematuria/leukocyturia / prostatitis question. Cheap, spot urine.
  11. Coagulation panel: PT/INR, aPTT, Fibrinogen, D-dimer — Required before TB-500 in a coronary plaque patient. TB-500 has angiogenic + actin-binding activity; plaque neovascularization is a known destabilization mechanism. This is a plaque-patient-specific safety gate that goes beyond Bachmeyer's general "TB-500 is not oncogenic" rebuttal.
  12. Uroflowmetry numerical results (Qmax, voided volume, PVR) + IPSS score — Closes the July 2025 Urology consult gap. Ehsan had the tests done but no numbers were reported.
  13. Homocysteine + B12 + Folate + Ferritin + Iron + TIBC + Transferrin saturation — Cardiovascular nutrient panel. Homocysteine is independently atherogenic; iron status informs ferritin-as-inflammation-marker interpretation.
  14. Ophthalmology baseline (visual acuity + fields + dilated fundoscopy) — Before long-term Tesamorelin. GHRH/GH-axis stimulation has a theoretical acromegalic risk profile over time; baseline optic disc / retina exam is the inexpensive gate.
  15. HBsAg + Anti-HCV — Cheap rule-out of viral hepatitis as co-driver of the fatty liver. Standard workup for any patient with imaging-confirmed steatosis before a metabolic protocol.

💉 GHK-Cu (Gly-His-Lys + Cu²⁺) — gene-level repair signal, not "just a skin peptide"

Cleared: CBC ✓.

Required before start: Serum copper Ceruloplasmin Zinc baseline (copper/zinc balance).

Mechanism per Bachmeyer masterclass: Endogenous tripeptide discovered 1973 (Dr. Loren Pickart, in young blood plasma). Declines ~60% with age: ~200 ng/mL at age 20 → ~80 ng/mL by age 60. Three major actions:

  • Gene expression reset — 2010 Hong et al. study showed GHK-Cu modulates 4,000+ genes, reactivating age-silenced repair genes and suppressing pathological ones.
  • Targeted copper delivery to mitochondrial Complex IV (cytochrome C oxidase) — the rate-limiting step of ATP synthesis. This is the mechanism Bachmeyer highlights as the most clinically important.
  • PGC-1α upregulation → mitochondrial biogenesis (new mitochondria, not just optimization of existing ones). Plus collagen + elastin + glycosaminoglycan synthesis stimulation. Plus SOD cofactor activity for antioxidant defense. Plus TNF-α and IL-6 suppression.

Ehsan-specific rationale: The mitochondrial biogenesis + Complex IV copper delivery mechanism makes GHK-Cu a natural pairing with MOTS-c and 5-Amino-1MQ in the mitochondrial-repair arm of the stack. The Schmorl node T10 + thoracic spondylosis findings add a connective-tissue repair rationale. Low CV-specific risk.
