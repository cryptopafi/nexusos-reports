Executive Summary

Three new flags from the 2026-04-13 panel reshape the protocol:

  1. AM cortisol 5.76 µg/dL sits at the floor of the 5.27–22.45 reference range. Functionally low — adrenal glands operating with no reserve, HPA axis is depleted or flatlined.
  2. DHEA-S 88 µg/dL is LOW for a 42-year-old male (expected 200–400). Adrenal androgen reserve depleted, consistent with low cortisol.
  3. Homocysteine 15.01 µmol/L sits just under the 16.2 upper limit. With B12 (661) and folate (14.5) both normal-to-high, the elevation points to MTHFR polymorphism or one-carbon metabolism cofactor gap (B2, B6, TMG) — not vitamin deficiency. With known LAD plaque, this is an active vascular inflammation driver.

Combined with prior findings (IGF-1 75 LOW, free T bottom, E2 46.95 HIGH, LDL 139, LAD plaque, 5-year LUTS), the clinical picture is:

  • HPA depletion layer (new): low cortisol + low DHEA-S.
  • Methylation/vascular layer (new): high-normal homocysteine + LAD plaque + LDL 139.
  • HPG axis layer (refined): LH 4.49 / FSH 5.81 normal + free T bottom + E2 47 HIGH → secondary hypogonadism from peripheral aromatase overconversion.
  • GH/metabolic layer (unchanged): IGF-1 75 LOW + fatty liver + LUTS + visceral fat drive.

Critical reconciliation — how v2.0 differs from v1: In v1 I proposed a sequenced Phase 0 with Tesamorelin deferred to Week 4. Cross-referencing both Bachmeyer notebooks revealed that the Extended notebook explicitly opposes phase-gating — GH analogues and Retatrutide are themselves foundational to HPA recovery and should run concurrently from Day 1. v2.0 therefore runs all peptides concurrently from Week 1, with strict Retatrutide titration (0.25 → 0.5 → 1 → 2 mg over weeks 1–12) and tight HPA monitoring at Week 2 and Week 6 as safety gates. If cortisol worsens rather than improves at Week 2, GH secretagogues are paused.

Eight structured queries were run — four on the primary notebook (Dr Trevor Bachmeyer) and four on the extended curated subset (Dr Trevor Bachmeyer Extended) — then reconciled for convergence, divergence, and silence. Key reconciled positions driving v2.0:

Position 1 — Concurrent, not gated

Extended notebook explicitly opposes phase-gating. Bachmeyer treats the "Three Biological Failures" (systemic inflammation, insulin resistance, ATP shortage) simultaneously. GH analogues and Retatrutide are themselves HPA-restorative. IGF-1 75 is too low to wait on.

  • DSIP mandatory cycling: 10 days ON / 5 days OFF (Extended notebook) to prevent tachyphylaxis and endogenous DSIP suppression
  • HPA monitoring at Week 2 and Week 6 (AM + PM cortisol): if cortisol declines rather than improves, pause Tesamorelin until rhythm returns

Position 2 — Retatrutide titration (Extended notebook, strict)

Bachmeyer's exact schedule: "0.25 to 0.5 to 1 to 2 to 2.4 to 5 over 16 to 20 weeks." Going faster produces "5% of the benefit and 300% increase in side effects."

  • Weeks 1–4: 0.25 mg/week
  • Weeks 5–8: 0.5 mg/week
  • Weeks 9–10: 1.0 mg/week
  • Weeks 11–12: 2.0 mg/week
  • (Weeks 13–16 would reach 2.4 mg; Weeks 17–20 reach 5 mg — beyond this protocol window)

Position 3 — SubQ only, no intranasal, no oral, no mixing

Extended notebook: "Sublingual doesn't work, intranasal doesn't work, oral doesn't work. SubQ period." Intranasal Selank/Semax is rejected. Syringe mixing is also absolute: "Different PH levels, different ion ratios... take some basic chemistry." Each peptide requires its own syringe. Peak-day injection count = 10–12 separate SubQ injections.

Position 4 — LAD plaque regression stack

BPC-157 500 mcg SubQ daily (40% atherosclerotic lesion reduction, cited), KPV 300–400 mcg SubQ daily (11% CAC reduction, 35% MACE reduction at 12 weeks), TB-500 5 mg SubQ every 5 days, MOTS-c 5 mg SubQ 2x/week (Monday + Thursday).

Position 5 — HPG restoration: Kisspeptin + Enclomiphene, HCG out, MT2 for libido

Kisspeptin-10 is Bachmeyer's "master control switch" for HPG restoration — preserves Leydig cells and fertility. HCG is rejected (Leydig burnout). Enclomiphene is standard-of-care but outside Bachmeyer's corpus — included as attending-physician addition.

For libido, Bachmeyer rejects PT-141 as a "band-aid" and uses Melanotan 2 (MT2) targeting MC4R in the medial preoptic area — 47% increase in erectile frequency/intensity + endothelial nitric oxide + anti-inflammatory effect.

Position 6 — E2 aromatase management

Primary driver is Retatrutide-driven visceral fat loss (~5% aromatase decrease per 1% body fat drop; target <15%, ideal 10% body fat). Secondary: DIM 300 mg/day — cited 30% reduction in circulating estrogen through enhanced phase 2 liver conjugation. Calcium-D-Glucarate and Grape Seed Extract are NOT in Bachmeyer's corpus — removed from protocol.

Position 7 — Methylation with MOTS-c, not oral cofactors alone

Methylated B-complex required even with normal B12/folate (primary notebook). Riboflavin 80 mg twice daily (primary). TMG and P5P dosing not specified in either notebook — standard clinical doses used. MOTS-c directly optimizes the folate cycle — the peptide mechanism for homocysteine clearance.
