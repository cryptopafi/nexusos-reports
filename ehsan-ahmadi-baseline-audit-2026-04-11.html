Four new flags from the April 2026 blood work

1. IGF-1 75.10 ng/mL LOW (ref 86.2–214)

For a 42M the expected range is ~120–170. At 75 he is ~55% below age-expected midpoint. This validates the clinical rationale for the GHRH stack but mandates a full pituitary panel (LH / FSH / Prolactin / Cortisol AM / FT4 / IGF-BP3) before dose titration. Differential: somatopause, GLP-1/caloric restriction effect from Mounjaro, hyperestradiolemia suppression of GH pulse, subclinical hypopituitarism (rule out).

2. Estradiol 46.95 pg/mL HIGH (ref ND–39.8)

Elevated E2 in a 42M with fatty liver + social alcohol + LAD plaque is independently atherogenic. Mechanism: adipose-tissue aromatase (CYP19A1) converts testosterone to estradiol; steatosis impairs hepatic E2 glucuronidation. Total-T / E2 ratio = 472.6 / 46.95 = 10.1 (optimal >15–20). High E2 also suppresses hepatic GH → IGF-1 axis, plausibly contributing to the low IGF-1.

3. LDL-C 139 mg/dL HIGH — CRITICAL in secondary-prevention context

AHA/ESC targets: LDL <70 mg/dL (class I), ideally <55 mg/dL for very-high-risk. Ehsan is ~2× his target. Mandates immediate high-intensity statin independent of peptide decisions.

4. Free Testosterone 13 pg/mL (bottom of 9.57–40.6 range)

Tier-1 Mandatory Tests — BLOCKERS Before Peptide Start

Ordered by safety priority. Ideally batched into one blood draw + one imaging appointment.

  1. hs-CRP — Inflammation biomarker. Per Bachmeyer: "CRP is 2.3× more predictive of cardiovascular death than LDL." Also the baseline against which BPC-157 / TB-500 / KPV anti-inflammatory claims are measured.
  2. apoB (apolipoprotein B) — Better than LDL-C for atherogenic particle count. Secondary-prevention target <65 mg/dL. Drives statin intensity and PCSK9 decision.
  3. Lp(a) — One-time genetic. ~20% of Europeans carry atherogenic levels independent of LDL. Standard-of-care in premature CAD.
  4. Coronary Artery Calcium Score (Agatston, gated cardiac CT) — Quantifies the LAD plaque on 0 → >1000 scale. Drives secondary-prevention intensity and gates TB-500 safety.
  5. IGF-BP3 — Required before any GHRH-based stack. IGF-1 / IGFBP-3 ratio is the bioactive readout; IGF-1 alone (measured: 75 LOW) is insufficient for safe titration.
  6. Amylase + Lipase — Required before Retatrutide. GLP-1 class pancreatitis baseline (standard FDA gate, not Bachmeyer-specific).
  7. Calcitonin (basal) — Required before Retatrutide. C-cell / MTC baseline for GLP-1 class (standard endocrinology gate, not Bachmeyer).
  8. PSA (total + free, with free/total ratio) — Required before GHRH stack AND for LUTS workup closure. 5-year LUTS at 42M without a PSA is a standing gap.
  9. Full pituitary panel: LH, FSH, Prolactin, FT4, FT3, Cortisol AM, DHEA-S — Required before Tesamorelin. Rules out hypopituitarism / secondary hypogonadism / pituitary adenoma as cause of low IGF-1 + low-normal Free T.
  10. Urine ACR + Urinalysis with microscopy — Required before Retatrutide (renal baseline) AND closes the LUTS hematuria/leukocyturia question.
  11. Coagulation panel: PT/INR, aPTT, Fibrinogen, D-dimer — Required before TB-500 in a coronary plaque patient.
  12. Uroflowmetry numerical results (Qmax, voided volume, PVR) + IPSS score — Closes the July 2025 Urology consult gap.
  13. Homocysteine + B12 + Folate + Ferritin + Iron + TIBC + Transferrin saturation — Cardiovascular nutrient panel. Homocysteine is independently atherogenic.
  14. Ophthalmology baseline (visual acuity + fields + dilated fundoscopy) — Before long-term Tesamorelin.
  15. HBsAg + Anti-HCV — Rule out viral hepatitis as co-driver of the fatty liver.
