Plain-Words Layer — what the red items mean

🔴 Anti-thyroglobulin antibodies 54.6 (high)
💬 Your immune system is making antibodies that attack the thyroid — an early autoimmune-thyroid signal (the Hashimoto's family). Right now your thyroid is keeping up, but the attack is the thing to calm down early, before it tires the gland out. Bachmeyer treats this as a fixable immune/inflammation problem, not a permanent thyroid disease.
🔴 Low estradiol (8.16) + low testosterone (0.26)
💬 Both of your sex hormones are low. Some of this can depend on where you are in your cycle (we don't have the cycle day), so the first step is a properly-timed re-test. If it's genuinely low, the approach is to gently restart your own hormone signaling rather than replace it.
🟠 Ferritin 29 (low for a cycling woman)
💬 Ferritin is your iron "savings account." 29 is technically "in range" but low for a menstruating woman, and low iron makes thyroid symptoms (fatigue, hair, cold) worse because iron is a cofactor the thyroid needs. Worth raising toward 70–100 with iron + vitamin C.
🟠 Vitamin D 31.4 (just at the floor)
💬 You're right at the bottom of "sufficient." Vitamin D helps calm autoimmune activity, so nudging it higher supports the thyroid plan.
🟡 FT3 / FT4 low-normal
💬 Your active thyroid hormones are at the low end of normal — consistent with the antibodies starting to drag on output. Fixing iron, selenium, and the immune signal usually lifts these.

Bachmeyer Protocol — Peptides: Full Dosing

Cross-referenced against Bachmeyer NLM (cb9cf1fd + 1eef6220) + LLM-Wiki. All injectables subcutaneous (SubQ); doses Bachmeyer-cited / standard-range, physician-set, start low. Nothing starts before the gating labs. The metabolic GLP-1 peptides are not used (metabolism is clean).

Tier 1 — Autoimmune triad (the core for the thyroid antibodies)

1. Thymosin Alpha-1 (TA-1) — immune rebalancer (the key peptide here)

  • Dose: 1.5–1.6 mg SubQ, 2×/week (or every 3rd day); cycle 4–12 weeks on (alt: 1 month on 5 days/wk, 3 months off, 3×/year).
  • Tracks: anti-thyroglobulin + anti-TPO antibodies (↓), symptoms.
💬 This is THE peptide for autoimmune thyroid — it's adaptogenic, meaning it calms an over-active immune attack (restores the "referee" T-cells) instead of revving it up. It directly targets the antibody production behind flag #1.

2. BPC-157 — gut–thyroid repair

  • Dose: 250–500 mcg/day SubQ (near the navel); cycle 5-on / 2-off advisable.
  • Tracks: symptoms + inflammation.
💬 Heals the gut lining — a leaky gut is one of the main drivers of thyroid autoimmunity, so sealing it removes fuel from the fire.

3. KPV — anti-inflammatory tripeptide

  • Dose: 500 mcg/day SubQ.
  • Tracks: hs-CRP, symptoms.
💬 Shuts down a specific inflammation switch (the NLRP3 / IL-1β pathway) that links gut inflammation to the thyroid attack. Completes Bachmeyer's autoimmune trio.
Tier 1.5 — Mitochondrial / energy (optional, Hashimoto's "ATP-deficit" leg)

4. MOTS-c — mitochondrial peptide

  • Dose: 5–10 mg SubQ, 1–3×/week, cycle 4–12 wk. Take WITH carbohydrates, never fasted (hypoglycemia risk).
💬 Optional energy booster for the cells — Bachmeyer frames Hashimoto's partly as an energy (ATP) shortage, and this helps the thyroid's "referee" immune cells work. Take it with food.

Tier 2 — Hormone Restoration

After a cycle-timed re-test confirms low E2/T.

5. Kisspeptin-10 — preferred, female HPG axis

  • Dose: 50–100 mcg SubQ, 3×/week (pulsatile).
  • Tracks: E2, testosterone, LH/FSH, libido/energy.
💬 The "master switch" that tells the brain to restart your own estrogen/testosterone cycle — Bachmeyer highlights it specifically for women (cycle regulation, PCOS, libido), and it preserves natural function instead of replacing hormones.

6. DSIP — optional, if sleep/cortisol/stress symptoms

  • Dose: 100–250 mcg SubQ, 30 min pre-bed, 2–4 wk cycles.
💬 A sleep-quality peptide that also calms the stress (cortisol) axis — useful if poor sleep or "wired-but-tired" is part of the picture, which is common with thyroid issues.
🚫 No HRT push / no thyroid-hormone-only fix as the primary plan — Bachmeyer's position is that replacing the hormone doesn't stop the autoimmune attack; fix the root (inflammation, gut, cofactors) first. 🚫 No metabolic GLP-1 peptides (metabolism is clean).
