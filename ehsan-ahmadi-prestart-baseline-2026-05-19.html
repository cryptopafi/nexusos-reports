Part B — Tesamorelin + Ipamorelin Stacking Resolution (Unchanged)

Q1. Is Tesa + Ipa concurrent consistent with Bachmeyer's rules?
No. Direct Extended-notebook quote: "Dr. Bachmeyer advises against mixing or stacking Tesamorelin and Ipamorelin together. His protocol dictates choosing one of them individually, or alternatively, running a specific synergistic pairing." Protocol v5 already encodes this: "Tesamorelin 1–2 mg SubQ nightly — Alternative to CJC/Ipa stack — never combine."

Q2. What two Bachmeyer-approved paths come closest to "fastest GH-axis stimulation"?

  • Path 1 — Tesamorelin monotherapy. FDA-approved 2010 for visceral adiposity (≈25% reduction). Single-receptor (GHRH-R) high-affinity stimulation.
  • Path 2 — CJC-1295 w/DAC + Ipamorelin combined. Bachmeyer's "synergy" pairing: CJC-1295 hits GHRH-R, Ipamorelin hits GHSR-1A. Parallel pathways → "40% greater GH elevation than either alone" + reduced tachyphylaxis.

Q3. Which path fits Ehsan's phenotype (fatty liver + visceral fat + LAD plaque + IGF-1 75.1 + IGFBP-3 3.26 LOW)?
Tesamorelin monotherapy — case is stronger with the new IGFBP-3 data. Four reasons: (a) Tesamorelin has the strongest documented epicardial / visceral fat reduction signal, addressing NAFLD + LAD-plaque substrate. (b) Single-axis stimulation is cleaner to titrate against known-low IGF-1 + IGFBP-3 in a patient with HPA-axis depletion. (c) Ipamorelin selectivity advantage is less valuable than Tesamorelin's visceral-fat indication here. (d) A GHRH analog acting at the pituitary somatotroph is the mechanistically correct first agent when the lesion appears at or upstream of the somatotroph — exactly the picture from IGF-1 + IGFBP-3 dual-low. Adding Ipamorelin before characterizing the pituitary risks confounding the signal.

Q4. Earliest safe switch-over from Tesa-solo → CJC+Ipa?
Bachmeyer NLM provides no numeric switch-over criterion. Corpus-defined cadence (v5 §+8w + Layer-2): Week 12, conditional on:

  • IGF-1 climbed from 75.1 toward ≥120 ng/mL.
  • IGFBP-3 climbed from 3.26 into ref range (≥3.3, target ≈5.0).
  • Pituitary MRI completed and negative, OR endocrinology disposition received.
  • HPA axis clarified (ACTH stim resolved; cortisol stable).
  • ALT/AST/FIB-4 not deteriorating.
  • E2 trending down (DIM or Enclomiphene in effect).
  • No systemic allergic reaction (Bachmeyer absolute, Primary cb9cf1fd).

If any fails at Week 12 → defer switch, continue Tesa-solo.
