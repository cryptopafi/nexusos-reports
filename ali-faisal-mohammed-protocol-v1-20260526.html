Peptide Protocol — What to Inject, Why, and When

💡
What are peptides? Peptides are short chains of amino acids — basically tiny protein fragments. Your body already uses them as signals. When injected, they tell specific cells to do specific jobs (repair, produce hormones, protect arteries). They work with your body's own systems, not instead of them. All peptides here are subcutaneous (just under the skin — like insulin injections for diabetics, with a tiny needle into belly fat).

Before starting Phase 2: Blood thickness (red blood cell count 5.6) needs to be confirmed below normal ceiling on a repeat test. If it's still elevated, Phase 2 is held until cleared. Phase 1 peptides are safe to start now.

Daily Supplement Protocol — Oral, No Injections

✅
Every supplement below is tied to a specific finding in your blood test. Nothing is added "just in case." Each one has a measurable target that will be confirmed at the 12-week retest.
Methylfolate (Active Vitamin B9)
400–800 µg daily · Morning with food
Why your blood test requires this
Your homocysteine is 12.2 (should be under 9) but your B12 is perfect at 914. This means the folate pathway specifically is the weak link — your body can't recycle homocysteine properly without it. Important: do not use regular "folic acid" (the cheap version in most supplements) — use only "methylfolate" or "5-MTHF" (the active form your body can actually use).
🎯 Target: Homocysteine under 9 µmol/L at 12 weeks
P-5-P (Active Vitamin B6)
50–100 mg daily · Morning
Why your blood test requires this
B6 is the other cofactor in the homocysteine recycling process. The active form "P-5-P" (pyridoxal-5-phosphate) bypasses the conversion step that some people struggle with. This works alongside methylfolate — together they attack the problem from two angles.
🎯 Target: Homocysteine under 9 µmol/L at 12 weeks. Don't exceed 100 mg/day.
TMG (Trimethylglycine / Betaine)
1,000–3,000 mg daily · With food, AM or split AM/PM
Why your blood test requires this
TMG is a "methyl donor" — it directly converts homocysteine into a useful amino acid via a completely separate pathway from B-vitamins. This means it works regardless of any genetic B-vitamin processing issues. It's the fastest-acting part of the homocysteine stack. Start at 1g, increase to 2g after 2 weeks (higher doses can cause loose stools in some people).
🎯 Target: Homocysteine under 9 µmol/L at 12 weeks
Vitamin D3 + K2 (MK-7 form)
D3: 3,000–4,000 IU · K2-MK7: 200 µg · With your largest meal (fat helps absorption)
Why your blood test requires this
Your Vitamin D is 45.7 ng/mL — technically in range, but Dr. Bachmeyer's optimal for testosterone and immune function is 60–80. The K2 (vitamin K2, MK-7 form) is paired with it because D3 increases calcium absorption, and K2 makes sure that calcium goes into bones — not into your artery walls. This is especially important given your blood calcium is very slightly elevated.
🎯 Target: Vitamin D 60–70 ng/mL at 8 weeks. ⚠ Check parathyroid hormone test first before going above 4,000 IU.
Omega-3 Fish Oil (EPA + DHA)
3,000–4,000 mg total omega-3s daily · Split across 2 meals
Why your blood test requires this
Your LDL is 128. Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA and DHA specifically — check the label) reduce the type of LDL that causes the most artery damage (small dense particles). Use triglyceride-form fish oil or algae oil — not the cheaper "ethyl ester" form that is less well absorbed.

Berberine

Calcium oxalate crystals were found in your urine — the first sign of potential kidney stones. Magnesium binds to oxalate (the building block of stones) inside your gut and urine, preventing it from combining with calcium to form crystals. This is the most evidence-backed intervention for stopping calcium oxalate stones before they form. Evening dosing also improves sleep quality and muscle recovery.
