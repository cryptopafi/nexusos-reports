Per-peptide rationale

Retatrutide — 92. Strongest evidence in his whole stack. Phase 2 RCT data: 22.8–24.2% weight loss at 48 wk, liver fat down up to ~86% (93% reached normal liver fat at 12 mg), with measured drops in triglycerides, LDL and blood pressure. This single drug attacks three of his core problems at once — obesity, fatty liver, dyslipidemia. Watch: GI tolerance + heart-rate bump (monitor given his LAD plaque).
Tesamorelin — 88. The only FDA-approved peptide here, Phase III in 806 patients, ~18% visceral-fat reduction, raises IGF-1, reduces liver fat. Directly targets his low IGF-1 / low IGFBP-3 (possible GH deficiency) plus visceral fat and fatty liver. The clean, single growth-hormone signal — keep it as the sole GHRH.
Enclomiphene — 82. ⏳ Well-characterized human SERM that raises the body's own testosterone and improves the testosterone:estrogen balance — which is exactly his pair of sex-hormone problems (Free-T at the floor = 13, E2 high = 46.95). High value, but correctly held pending the 16.06 re-draw: start 12.5 mg/day only if E2 still >40 or Free-T still <15.
MOTS-c — 58 · Kisspeptin-10 — 55 · 5-Amino-1MQ — 52. Middle tier: mechanistically relevant to his metabolic / hormonal axes but riding on animal or early-human data, not real trials. Keep MOTS-c and 5-Amino as weak metabolic adjuncts (take MOTS-c with carbs). Kisspeptin only as an add-on if Enclomiphene's testosterone response is inadequate.
CJC-1295 w/DAC — 50 · Ipamorelin — 50. His planned "Layer 2" pair. Ipamorelin is a clean partner; CJC-1295 is a second growth-hormone doorbell that is redundant on top of Tesamorelin — use it only as a Tesa alternative, never stacked. Both stay deferred to Week 12 and gated on labs.
