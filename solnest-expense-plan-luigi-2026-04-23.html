1. Technology Build — ai-anima.ai €215,000RPT
Category 01 · Largest Line · 41% of base burn
Technology Build — ai-anima.ai (external vendor)
€215,000
What it covers

MVP (Minimum Viable Product) build by external vendor ai-anima.ai across months M1–M9 (€185,000), followed by platform maintenance M10–M12 (€30,000). This is the entire software build budget. Solnest has no internal engineering headcount — all product development is outsourced to ai-anima.ai under a formal SOW (Statement of Work).

Why we need it

Building a two-sided solar-installation marketplace requires a functioning platform: installer onboarding, buyer lead capture, quoting workflow, payment escrow, and a CER (Comunità Energetiche Rinnovabili — Italian renewable energy community) module. Pre-revenue, the most capital-efficient path is a single specialist vendor rather than hiring an engineering team.

How we sized it

€185,000 for M1-M9 build (taper-profiled: lower in M1-M2, peak M4-M7, wind-down M8-M9) + €30,000 annual maintenance from M10. Benchmark: comparable Italian marketplace MVP builds with 9-month delivery range €150K–€250K from specialist shops. Single-vendor concentration is RISK-01 in the risk register — mitigated by IP assignment agreement and source-code escrow in Solnest's name.

Investor angle

Disclosed RPT (Related Party Transaction): ai-anima.ai is also owned by the Angel Investor. A formal SOW with IP assignment and source-code escrow is a pre-condition of any fund release (Gate 2). One competing CEE (Central and Eastern European) quote is required before final SOW sign-off (V1 verification item). Without a binding SOW this line remains ANGEL_BLOCKED.

2. Founder Compensation €76,800
Category 02
Founder Compensation — Pafi + Leo (Italian SRL employees)
€76,800
What it covers

Gross salary of €30,000/year each for Pafi and Leo, employed through the Italian SRL (Società a Responsabilità Limitata — the Italian limited liability company). The loaded figure of €76,800 applies the mandatory 1.28× Italian employer burden: INPS (Istituto Nazionale della Previdenza Sociale — the Italian social security body) contributions (~30%), INAIL workplace insurance, and TFR (Trattamento di Fine Rapporto — the Italian statutory severance accrual) reserve.

Why we need it

Founders must be on payroll to work legally inside the Italian SRL. The 1.28× loaded factor is non-negotiable under Italian national labour law — it applies to every employee regardless of company size. Founders are not contractors.

How we sized it

€30,000 gross × 2 founders × 1.28 loaded factor = €76,800. Benchmark: Italian pre-seed founder compensation typically ranges €24K–€48K gross; we sit at the lower end. This is confirmed in LB-03 (Angel-Blocked item 3 — awaiting written confirmation from Angel Investor).

Investor angle

Founders at ~40% of market rate for their roles is the strongest trust signal available at pre-seed. Combined with €180,000 founder cash already committed to the balance sheet, this reads as full operational commitment. Below-market comp is a standard Series A due-diligence screen — we pass it cleanly.

3. Sales & Market Entry €57,000
Category 03
Sales & Market Entry — BDM Contractor + Installer Network
€57,000
What it covers

Business development lead (BDM — Business Development Manager) on Partita IVA (Italian VAT contractor status) at €6,000/month from M3, contracted through month M12. Plus €6,000 for installer-network development costs: travel to site visits, demo events, and LOI (Letter of Intent) facilitation with target installation companies across northern Italy.

Why we need it

Solnest is a two-sided marketplace. Without a supply-side BDM physically signing up vetted installers in Milan and surrounding provinces, the platform has no listing inventory. MGC (Milano Green Consulting — the Angel Investor's solar holding) brings an initial installer cohort, but organic supply-side growth requires a dedicated field role from M3 onward.

How we sized it

BDM: €6,000/month × 10 months (M3–M12) = €60,000 contractor invoices — minus Partita IVA flat-tax adjustment = effective €51,000 net cost. Installer network: €6,000 covering site-visit travel, trade-show presence, and LOI paperwork. BDM contractor status is maintained throughout Y1 with a documented secondary client to satisfy Italian anti-abuse rules. Conversion to full employee is planned for Y2 post-revenue.

Investor angle

The BDM staying as a contractor in Y1 is a deliberate structural choice, not a gap. The anti-abuse mitigation (documented secondary client) is in place. Series A auditors will look for a conversion plan — we have one (Y2, post first-revenue cohort). The installer network €6K is a growth-phase cost centre: each LOI signed is the supply-side pipeline feeding the demand-side funnel.

4. Marketing €35,000
Category 04
Marketing — Italian PV Market, Demand-Side Acquisition
€35,000
What it covers

Demand-side buyer acquisition for the Italian solar-installation market (PV — Photovoltaic). Digital channels: Google Ads on high-intent search terms (impianto fotovoltaico, installatore solare Milano), LinkedIn for C&I (Commercial & Industrial) SMB prospects, and retargeting. Ramped from M3 when the MVP demo environment is live enough to send traffic to.

Why we need it

A two-sided marketplace with supply (installers) but no demand (buyer leads) does not close transactions. Marketing spend is the demand-side investment. Without it, the BDM has nothing to show installers when pitching Solnest as a lead source.

How we sized it

€35,000 across M3–M12 = ~€3,500/month average. Back-loaded: M3–M5 at ~€2,000/month (brand + testing), M6–M12 at ~€4,000/month (performance). Benchmark: Italian SaaS marketplace peers at pre-seed typically run €2,000–€5,000/month digital spend in Y1. We are at the mid-range.

Investor angle

Marketing is ramped to match platform readiness — no spend before the product is ready to convert. This is conservative and investor-friendly. Series A will want to see early CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) data from the M6–M12 spend period.

5. Legal & Entity Setup €28,300
Category 05
Legal & Entity Setup — Ireland HoldCo + Italy SRL
€28,300
What it covers

One-time and early-year costs to establish the dual-entity structure: Irish Limited Company (HoldCo) incorporation, Italy SRL incorporation via notaio (Italian notary — mandatory for SRL formation), SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) note documentation, inter-company service agreement between Irish HoldCo and Italian SRL, IP (Intellectual Property) assignment from founders to HoldCo, and source-code escrow agreement with ai-anima.ai.

Why we need it

Option 1 (recommended structure) requires two legal entities across two jurisdictions. Each entity requires qualified local counsel. The SAFE note is Irish-law governed and must be drafted by a Dublin-qualified solicitor. The IP assignment is critical — without it, Solnest does not legally own its own product.

How we sized it

Dublin solicitor: ~€12,000 (HoldCo formation + SAFE + interco agreement). Milan corporate counsel: ~€10,000 (SRL formation, employment contracts, initial advice). Notaio (notary) fees for SRL: ~€3,300. IP assignment + source-code escrow setup: ~€3,000. Benchmark: Dublin SAFE documentation €8K–€15K; Milan SRL notaio €2,500–€4,500.

Investor angle

Having named counsel in both jurisdictions before a Series A shortens due-diligence by two to three weeks and removes a common deal-delay point. IP clearly in HoldCo ownership is the single most important legal hygiene item for any future M&A or secondary transaction.

6. Compliance — AML/KYC, NIS2, GDPR €27,500
Category 06
Compliance — AML/KYC, NIS2, GDPR
€27,500
What it covers

External DPO (Data Protection Officer) retainer for GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) compliance; NIS2 (EU Network and Information Security Directive v2, transposed in Italy October 2024) readiness assessment — applies because Solnest touches energy-infrastructure data; AML/KYC (Anti-Money Laundering / Know Your Customer) framework for installer identity verification and sanctions screening; and a baseline information-security policy set.

Why we need it

Processing installer and customer personal data at marketplace scale without a designated DPO is a direct GDPR exposure — fines at €20M or 4% of revenue. NIS2 applies to platforms in the energy-adjacent sector. AML/KYC is operationally required: marketplace platforms handling money flows must verify counterparty identity and screen against EU sanctions lists.

How we sized it

External DPO retainer: €12,000/year (fractional, covers monthly advisory + incident response protocol). NIS2 readiness engagement (Q3): €8,000 one-time assessment. AML/KYC framework build: €7,500 (legal + tech implementation). Benchmark: external DPO retainers for SMEs in Italy and Ireland range €8K–€18K/year.

Investor angle

Most pre-seed companies defer compliance and get caught at Series A. A DPO in place and NIS2 readiness documented removes two of the most common due-diligence friction points — outright. Series A investors in climate-tech and fintech-adjacent verticals specifically check these boxes.

7. Regulatory & Legal (ongoing) €22,000
Category 07
Regulatory & Legal — Ongoing Counsel, TP Memo
€22,000
What it covers

Annual TP (Transfer Pricing) memorandum documenting the arm's-length pricing between the Italian SRL and the Irish HoldCo under OECD guidelines (€9,000); Irish economic-substance filing (€4,000); ongoing Italian corporate counsel retainer covering commercial terms, employment contract updates, and general legal questions arising during operations (€9,000/year).

Why we need it

Two-entity structures across EU jurisdictions require an annual TP memo. Without it, the Agenzia delle Entrate (Italy's tax authority) can re-attribute profit from Ireland to Italy and issue back-taxes plus penalties. The Airbnb (€576M settlement, 2023) and Uber Eats Italy (judicial administration, 2020) cases are the documented enforcement precedents.

How we sized it

TP memo: €9,000 — market rate for a pre-revenue / early-revenue company. Irish economic-substance filing: €4,000 (annual, required since BEPS 2.0 — Base Erosion and Profit Shifting). Ongoing Italian counsel: €9,000 = ~€750/month retainer for general corporate matters. No retainer for Dublin solicitor — on-call only (ad hoc billed under Cat 05 setup budget if needed).

Investor angle

A signed TP memo in the data room is explicitly requested by Series A auditors reviewing dual-jurisdiction European structures. Filed in Y1 rather than retroactively removes weeks of due-diligence risk. Under BEPS 2.0 enforcement timelines, Ireland is no longer a "soft" jurisdiction for substance — this line is genuinely required, not optional.

8. G&A €18,500
Category 08
General & Administrative — Co-working, Equipment, Operations
€18,500
What it covers

Shared co-working desks in Milan (€16,500 — two desks for the core team, used for the in-person sprint week each month plus full-time BDM base); developer equipment amortised over three years (€2,000 contribution in Y1 for two laptops); registered-office fees for the Irish HoldCo; CCIAA (Camera di Commercio, Industria, Artigianato e Agricoltura — Italian Chamber of Commerce) annual fee (~€90); and unallocated miscellaneous operational costs.

Why we need it

The engineering and product work is done collaboratively: founders work together in Milan one week per month and the BDM needs a professional base. No vanity office — co-working is flex. The Irish HoldCo requires a registered address in Dublin.

How we sized it

€16,500/year co-working (2 desks × ~€700/month in central Milan — below co-working market rate of €800–€1,200/month for comparable). €2,000 equipment. Held to the absolute minimum operationally defensible level.

Investor angle

No dedicated office. No vanity real estate. An auditor reading this line will note capital discipline — one of the highest-variance line items at pre-seed, and we are at the floor.

9. Insurance €15,500
Category 09
Insurance — RC Prof, Cyber, D&O
€15,500
What it covers

RC Professionale (Italian professional liability insurance — mandatory for service businesses operating under Italian law, €5,000/year); cyber insurance covering data-breach liability and business interruption (€6,000/year); D&O (Directors & Officers) insurance covering the board from personal liability in company decisions (€4,500/year — activates when the Angel Investor formally takes a board seat).

Why we need it

RC Prof is legally mandated and non-negotiable for the Italian SRL. Cyber insurance is effectively required given Solnest handles personal data and payment flows — any breach without cover becomes a balance-sheet event. D&O is standard governance for any company accepting institutional capital: no professional investor will sit on a board without it.

How we sized it

RC Prof €5,000/year (market: €3,500–€7,000 for Italian marketplace businesses). Cyber €6,000/year (market: €4,000–€10,000 for pre-revenue SaaS/marketplace with payment flows). D&O €4,500/year (market: €3,000–€6,000 for two-director pre-revenue companies). LB-06 (Angel-Blocked item 6): D&O activation depends on Angel Investor confirming board-seat acceptance.

Investor angle

Insurance in a data room signals operational maturity. Series A term sheets for climate-tech and marketplace verticals routinely require RC Prof, cyber cover, and D&O as conditions precedent. Having all three in Y1 removes negotiation friction at closing.

10. AI Agents (v1.0 ramp) €12,240
Category 10 · v1.0 addition over v0.1.2 baseline
AI Agents — LLM APIs, Vector DB, Automation Workflows
€12,240
What it covers

LLM (Large Language Model) API usage fees — Anthropic Claude (primary), OpenAI (fallback) — for inference powering the platform's AI-assisted workflows: installer lead qualification, document parsing for permits and certifications, and buyer-onboarding automation. Plus vector-database hosting (for semantic search of installer capabilities) and internal automation-agent infrastructure costs.

Why we need it

AI agents replace what would otherwise require three additional human roles: lead qualifier, document reviewer, and onboarding coordinator. Without this line, headcount has to grow or these workflows run manually — neither is defensible at pre-seed burn rates. This is the operating-leverage bet of the build.

How we sized it

Ramped from €400/month in Q1 (low volume, MVP testing) to €1,600/month in Q4 (live platform, growing workflow coverage). Annual total: €12,240. This line is the v1.0 delta — it is not in the v0.1.2 base workbook and is added to reach the v1.0 target of €639,360. Contingency is not recalculated on the AI Agents addition; the 20% contingency is held at the v0.1.2 base level.

Investor angle

A dedicated AI spend line, ramped not flat, signals this is a real cost centre rather than a footnote. The ramp profile shows usage discipline — spend follows product adoption, not aspirational projections. Series A reviewers in the 2025–2026 vintage specifically look for this structure in AI-enabled marketplace pitches.

11. Payment Infrastructure €8,000
Category 11
Payment Infrastructure — Escrow, Stripe, SEPA, Fraud Screening
€8,000
What it covers

Monthly floor costs for the payment stack: Stripe monthly minimums and SEPA (Single Euro Payments Area) direct-debit processing for Italian buyers; reconciliation tooling; fraud-screening layer; and the escrow-provider setup fee and retainer for marketplace transactions. Variable transaction fees are inside the gross-margin model, not this fixed-cost line.

Why we need it

Solnest handles real money flows between buyers and vetted solar installers. Per-project transaction values run €5,000–€50,000. An escrow layer is operationally non-negotiable: without it, neither party trusts the platform with their money. Stripe is the baseline; SEPA is required because Italian SMB buyers pay by bank transfer, not card.

How we sized it

~€670/month average across the year: Stripe base + SEPA processing (€350/month), fraud screening (€200/month), escrow retainer (€120/month). Total €8,000/year. This is the floor — transaction-volume-based fees sit in gross margin assumptions.

Investor angle

Marketplaces without a documented escrow story lose Series A conversations early. This line buys a clean answer. The separation of fixed infrastructure cost from variable transaction fees signals financial model sophistication.

12. Localization €7,500
Category 12
Localization — Italian Copywriting, Legal Translation
€7,500
What it covers

Professional Italian copywriting for all product surfaces, onboarding flows, and marketing pages (part-time freelancer); certified legal translation of contract templates between Italian and English — installer agreements, GDPR notices, and terms of service (ongoing retainer); and translation of investor and reporting documents for dual-jurisdiction governance.

Why we need it

The operating market is Italy. Solar installation buyers and Italian SMB installers have zero tolerance for machine-translated or anglicised copy — it signals a non-native product immediately. Legal templates must be certified-translated to be enforceable in Italian courts and readable by the Italian commercialista (accountant) and notaio (notary).

How we sized it

~€625/month: €350/month part-time Italian copywriter, €275/month certified legal-translation retainer. Total €7,500/year. Benchmark: Italian freelance copywriter rates €300–€600/month part-time; certified legal translation €200–€400/month retainer for a tech company.

Investor angle

Italian-first product without translator-English is a visible differentiator against remote competitors building generic solar platforms. This line is a direct competitive moat investment, not a cost.

€6,000

€250/month data-room software + cap-table tool = €3,000/year. Intentionally lean — no full-time investor-relations function at pre-seed. Quarterly reporting is founder-written. Data room is Solnest's primary due-diligence artefact for this SAFE transaction.

Investor angle

A data room built and maintained from M1 shortens Series A due-diligence by one to two weeks and signals operational readiness to sophisticated LPs (Limited Partners) behind the lead Series A fund.
