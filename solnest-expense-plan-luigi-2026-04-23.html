1. Team & People Costs €126,800
Category 01
Team & People Costs
€126,800
What it covers

Founder compensation (Pafi + Leo) and the BDM (Business Development Manager) contractor fee for Year 1. There is no in-house engineering team — software is built end-to-end by external vendor ai-anima.ai under the Technology Build line (Category 4). Loaded cost on founder compensation includes INPS (Istituto Nazionale della Previdenza Sociale — Italian social-security authority) + INAIL (Istituto Nazionale Assicurazione Infortuni sul Lavoro — Italian workplace-injury insurance) + TFR (Trattamento di Fine Rapporto — Italian end-of-contract severance) at 1.28× factor.

Why we need it

Founders need minimum viable compensation to remain full-time on Solnest. The BDM builds the Italian installer supply side through in-person signings from Month 3. The ai-anima vendor model eliminates the need for in-house engineers, preserving runway for product build and market entry.

How we sized it

Pafi: €2,500/mo gross × 1.28 = €3,200/mo = €38,400/yr. Leo: €2,500/mo gross × 1.28 = €3,200/mo = €38,400/yr. Founders combined: €76,800 (employer-side loaded; €60,000 gross take-home combined). BDM contractor on Partita IVA (Italian VAT-registered self-employment number): M3 €2K → M4 €3K → M5 €4K → M6 €5K → M7+ €6K/mo = €50,000 total. Team total: €76,800 + €50,000 = €126,800.

Investor angle

Founders at €30,000/year gross each (below 50% of market) is the strongest trust-signal at pre-seed. Combined with €180,000 founder cash already on the balance sheet, this reads as commitment. BDM contractor conversion to employee is planned for Y2. No internal engineering headcount means the bulk of the technology spend appears in Category 4 (€215,000) — not payroll.

2. Commercialista / Accounting €24,000
Category 02
Commercialista / Accounting
€24,000
What it covers

Monthly bookkeeping, quarterly VAT (Value Added Tax) filings, annual corporate tax return, payroll run, and year-end statutory financial statements in both Italy and Ireland. In Italy this role holds statutory reporting responsibility equivalent to a certified public accountant.

Why we need it

Italy runs on a monthly VAT cadence and the Agenzia delle Entrate expects perfect bookkeeping from day one. Missed filings trigger automatic penalties regardless of company size.

How we sized it

€1,500/month Italian commercialista retainer + €500/month Irish bookkeeper. Benchmarks: Milan SRL (Società a Responsabilità Limitata — Italian limited liability company) bookkeeping €1,200–€2,000/month; Dublin holding-company bookkeeping €400–€700/month. Full fee stack includes year-end statement preparation.

Investor angle

A Series A data room without monthly reconciled books is a deal-killer. This budget line is what makes that data room deliverable.

3. Legal (Italian + Irish Dual Counsel) €36,000
Category 03
Legal — Dual Counsel
€36,000
What it covers

Italian corporate counsel for the operating company (incorporation, employment contracts, commercial terms, general counsel retainer) plus Irish solicitor for the holding entity (formation, share-allocation resolutions, SAFE note documentation, inter-company service agreement).

Why we need it

The two-entity structure needs coordinated legal work on both sides. The SAFE note is Irish-law governed and must be drafted by a Dublin-qualified solicitor.

How we sized it

€24,000/year Italian retainer (€2,000/month) + €12,000/year Irish counsel covering formation and transaction work. Benchmarks: Milan mid-tier corporate retainers €18K–€36K/year; Dublin SAFE-note work €8K–€15K.

Investor angle

Having named counsel in both jurisdictions before closing a Series A shortens due-diligence by two to three weeks.

4. Technology Build — ai-anima.ai External Vendor €215,000
Category 04
Technology Build (ai-anima)
€215,000
What it covers

End-to-end product development contracted to ai-anima.ai, an external software-development vendor. This single line covers everything that would otherwise require an in-house engineering team: platform architecture, front-end and back-end development, integrations, testing, and post-launch maintenance. MVP (Minimum Viable Product) development runs M1–M9 (€185,000); maintenance retainer runs M9–M12 (€30,000). No internal Italian engineers — software is built entirely externally under a fixed-budget SOW (Statement of Work).

Why we need it

Solnest has no in-house engineers at pre-seed. The ai-anima engagement is the only path to a live marketplace by Month 9. The vendor model reduces hiring risk, eliminates Italian payroll loading on engineering seats, and caps technology spend at a contractually agreed ceiling.

How we sized it

MVP sprints M1–M9: M1 €18K → peak M3–M6 €22K → M9 €18K = €185,000 base. Maintenance M9–M12: €7,500/mo × 4 = €30,000. Total: €215,000. Sprint cadence from P&L Monthly sheet, row "Tech build MVP via ai-anima.ai (M1-M9)". Driver range: Base €185K, Pessimistic €220K, Optimistic €150K. Average sprint: €185,000 ÷ 9 = €20,556/mo.

Investor angle

€215K for a complete marketplace build via SOW is far cheaper than hiring 3–4 Italian engineers (€240K–€350K in loaded salary alone, plus management overhead). The fixed-budget contract provides a cost ceiling. Series A auditors reading this line should understand: this IS the engineering budget, not a supplement to headcount.

7. Regulatory & Transfer Pricing €15,000
Category 07
Regulatory & Transfer Pricing
€15,000
What it covers

Transfer-pricing memorandum between Italian operating company and Irish holding (required annually under OECD (Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development) guidelines), economic-substance filing in Ireland, and sector-specific registrations for operating a solar-adjacent marketplace in Italy.

Why we need it

Two-entity structures across EU jurisdictions require a transfer-pricing memo justifying profit allocation. Without it the Italian tax authority can re-attribute profit and issue back-taxes with penalties.

How we sized it

€8,000 transfer-pricing memo (market rate for pre-revenue/early-revenue company), €4,000 Irish economic-substance filing, €3,000 Italian sector registrations and annual renewals.

Investor angle

A signed transfer-pricing memo in the data room is specifically requested by Series A auditors. Filed in Y1 rather than retroactively = multiple weeks of negotiation time saved.

9. Compliance (GDPR, DPO, NIS2 — Network and Information Security Directive 2) €14,000
Category 09
Compliance
€14,000
What it covers

External data-protection-officer (DPO) retainer (required under GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) for companies processing personal data at scale), NIS2 compliance engagement (Solnest touches energy-infrastructure data), and a baseline information-security policy set.

Why we need it

Processing installer and customer data at scale without a designated DPO is a direct regulatory exposure. GDPR treats this as a fineable offense at €20M or 4% of revenue, whichever is higher.

How we sized it

€8,000/year external DPO retainer, €4,000 NIS2 readiness assessment in Q3, €2,000 information-security policy templates and annual review.

Investor angle

Most pre-seed companies defer this and get caught at Series A. Having it in Y1 removes a due-diligence friction point entirely.

Why not the cheaper options?

The cheapest option — Irish-only entity with Italian contractors and no declared Italian presence — comes in at €566,000 Year 1. It was rejected. Italian enforcement history is clear:

  • Airbnb settled €576 million with the Agenzia delle Entrate in 2023 for exactly this pattern (operating in Italy without a declared permanent establishment).
  • Uber Eats Italy was placed in judicial administration in 2020 over its contractor-based logistics force.
  • Estonian variant loses to Ireland on founder-extraction math: 42.3% total tax leakage (Estonia path) vs. 35.3% (Ireland path) at €200K/year distribution-to-founder profile.
