Q4

Reg-CF Regulatory · 2025-2026 Updates

Audit threshold: UNCHANGED

The $1.235M threshold remains in force. No 2025 or 2026 changes confirmed. SBCFAC proposed raising the reviewed-statement cap to $250K, but this is pending SEC consideration only, not enacted.

Financial statement tiers (current, unchanged):

  • At or below $618K: Unaudited and certified
  • $618K to $1.235M: Reviewed (first-time issuer), audited (repeat)
  • Above $1.235M: Audited required

SolNest targeting $1.2M falls just under the audit threshold, so reviewed financials are sufficient. No change from D4 analysis.

Form C disclosure: UNCHANGED

No new 2025-2026 Form C disclosure additions found. March 2026 SEC guidance clarified the rolling 12-month $5M cap calculation: administrative clarification only, not a new cost burden.

Foreign founder (Romanian citizen, Delaware C-Corp)

NO BLOCKER CONFIRMED. Reg-CF permits foreign issuers if organized under US laws (Delaware C-Corp qualifies). Standard eligibility: US-incorporated entity, not an investment company, must file Form C. Romanian citizenship of the sole founder does NOT disqualify. Bad actor disqualification applies only to securities violations: a Romanian citizen with no US criminal record is CLEAR.

The only citizenship risk confirmed in prior research (R2) is SBIR and STTR programs, which are separate from Reg-CF.

HIGH confidence Multiple primary SEC sources confirm; no contradicting data found

Contradictions and Unresolved

  1. Platform average vs sector average. Republic's $500K average does not equal pre-revenue solar average. D4 used sector-specific EDGAR data. The "Republic is a good platform" narrative is based on all-sector data and misleads.
  2. Raise Green as cleantech crowdfunding alternative. D4 mentioned this as a 2027 option. Post-merger with Honeycomb, Raise Green is a project-finance lending platform. Recommending it for a SaaS startup would be a category error.
  3. DOGE savings claims. DOGE claimed $2T savings; actual approximately $1.4B audited. DOE programs disrupted regardless. Both the political narrative (harm to cleantech) and the fiscal reality (small actual savings) are confirmed simultaneously.

  5. SeedBlink EU entity requirement. Unclear whether a Delaware C-Corp with a Romanian citizen as sole founder can raise on SeedBlink WITHOUT forming an EU entity. Most likely requires EU entity. Flagged for legal verification before 2027 action.
