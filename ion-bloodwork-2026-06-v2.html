Plain-Words Layer — what the red items mean

🔴 Insulin resistance (HOMA-IR 16, insulin 56.6, glucose 116)
💬 In plain words: Your body still keeps blood sugar near-normal, but only because the pancreas pumps out ~6× more insulin than it should to get there. Like flooring the gas pedal just to drive at normal speed — the engine is straining and will eventually tire out. This is the single most important thing to fix, and the good news is it's very reversible with diet, movement, weight loss, and the metabolic peptides below.
🔴 Gamma-GT 356 (6.5× the upper limit), with normal AST/ALT
💬 In plain words: GGT is a liver "stress" enzyme. It's very high while the other two liver enzymes are normal — a pattern that usually points to fatty liver and/or alcohol/oxidative load, not active liver damage yet. Needs a liver ultrasound and 90 days off alcohol. It typically falls fast once insulin resistance and visceral fat come down.
🔴 Eosinophils 10.8% (high)
💬 In plain words: These are immune cells that rise with allergies or a parasite. Before starting any immune-affecting peptide, a simple stool test + an IgE blood test should rule out a parasite — cheap, important, and easy.
🔴 Low free testosterone (14) + low estradiol (<10)
💬 In plain words: The "active" form of testosterone your tissues can actually use is slightly low, and so is estrogen. Your total testosterone is okay, so this is mostly downstream of the insulin/metabolic problem, not a primary testicle failure. As metabolic health improves, these usually recover on their own — so we fix the root first rather than jumping to testosterone injections.
🟡 MCV 98 (just over) & kidney filtration 86 (mildly under 90)
💬 In plain words: Both are only a hair outside the line and not alarming on their own. Worth a recheck and a look at B12/folate (for the slightly large red cells) and hydration/kidney over time.

Bachmeyer Protocol — Peptides (informational)

Sequencing follows Bachmeyer's metabolic-first logic. Nothing starts before the pre-peptide workup clears (see gating below).

Tier 1 — Metabolic core

Retatrutide (triple GLP-1/GIP/glucagon agonist)
💬 In plain words: Bachmeyer's top pick here. It attacks fat (especially the dangerous liver/belly fat), lowers blood sugar and insulin, and helps the liver clear fat — exactly the three problems on this panel at once. Slow monthly dose steps to avoid nausea/dehydration. He explicitly prefers it over semaglutide/tirzepatide for this insulin-resistant profile (those work on fewer pathways and ~70% of weight returns after stopping).
MOTS-c (mitochondrial peptide)
💬 In plain words: Think of it as a tune-up for the cell's "batteries" so muscles burn sugar better — it directly improves insulin sensitivity and pairs naturally with exercise.
BPC-157 (repair/anti-inflammatory)
💬 In plain words: A gut- and tissue-healing peptide that calms inflammation. Used here mainly to support the gut–liver axis. Cycle it (e.g. 5 days on / 2 off) rather than running it open-ended.

Tier 2 — Adjuncts (after Tier 1 is tolerated)

Cardarine / GW-501516 (PPAR-δ agonist — not a peptide)
💬 In plain words: Helps the body prefer fat for fuel and nudges cholesterol/endurance in a good direction. Use in defined cycles, not continuously.
KPV (anti-inflammatory tripeptide)
💬 In plain words: A gentle inflammation-calmer (a fragment of a natural hormone). Helpful for the elevated CRP — but only after the eosinophil/parasite workup is clean, since it touches the immune system.

Tier 3 — Growth-hormone secretagogues → DEFERRED

💬 In plain words: Peptides like CJC-1295/Ipamorelin/Tesamorelin raise IGF-1. Ion's IGF-1 is already mid-normal (129), so these are not needed and could even worsen insulin resistance if over-pushed. Skip for now.

Hormone restoration — natural-first

Kisspeptin (preferred)
💬 In plain words: The "ignition key" that tells the brain to restart the body's own testosterone production — it preserves natural function instead of replacing it. Bachmeyer's first choice over TRT.
Enclomiphene (alternative)
💬 In plain words: A pill that nudges the pituitary to make more of its own testosterone signal. Good fit for "secondary" low-T like this. Reserve if free T stays low after the metabolic fix.
🚫 No first-line TRT (testosterone injections shut down your own production) and 🚫 no aromatase inhibitors (estradiol is already low — blocking it further would backfire). Both are Bachmeyer positions.

Pre-Peptide Workup (gating — do these first)

  • Hepatic ultrasound ± FibroScan — confirm fatty liver / fibrosis stage
  • Coproparasitology + total IgE — clear the eosinophilia before immune-modulators
  • TSH / FT4 / FT3 — thyroid contribution to metabolism
  • ApoB + Lp(a) — true cardiovascular risk beyond LDL
  • Homocysteine, B12, folate, vitamin D — methylation + deficiencies
  • 90-day alcohol abstinence, then re-draw GGT
