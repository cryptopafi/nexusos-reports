Dubai luxury market shows predictable seasonality: HNWI buyers absent May-September (summer). Entry window: May-September 2026 — reduced competition, motivated sellers (including geopolitical-hesitant investors), potentially 5-8% negotiation premium. Exit: October-March when European and Asian buyer demand peaks.

Iran Strikes on UAE/Gulf — Timeline

  • Early March 2026: Iran strikes trigger; Dubai Financial Market benchmark -4.65% at open on March 4
  • First half of March: Property transactions -25% (8,199 to 6,129 units per two-week period) — WorldPropertyJournal
  • Mid-March: DFM Real Estate Index -20% in 5 trading sessions, erasing all YTD (year-to-date) gains
  • Transaction volume: -37% YoY in mid-March; luxury villa/waterfront "quiet price trimming"
  • Diplomatic: Tehran-Abu Dhabi ties severed; 15-20% price corrections among leveraged investors
  • April 2026: Ceasefire announced; deferred transactions executing; meaningful market rebound expected

Structural vs. Transient Impact

Industry consensus (BusinessToday, RamiPropertyInspections, GajProperties): the slowdown is sentiment-driven, not fundamental. Dubai's structural advantages — 0% income tax, infrastructure, regulatory stability, Golden Visa — remain intact. HNWI buyers showed "48-72 hour pause" behaviour, not exit behaviour. Leveraged and new investors were more vulnerable.

Competing safe-haven risk: Guardian (April 7, 2026) reports Milan flat-tax haven emerging as alternative for super-wealthy, partially diverting HNWI capital. Monitor for sustained competition.
