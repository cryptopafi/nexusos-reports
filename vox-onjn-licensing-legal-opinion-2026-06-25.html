1 What ONJN decided

VOX applied for a Class-II affiliate licence to send promotional SMS for licensed RO gambling operators to recipients in VOX's own database, with dedicated redirect links, under affiliation contracts. ONJN rejected it and held the mechanism itself is impermissible — neither via a Class-II licence nor freely outside the regime.

  • Not "affiliation" in the regulatory sense — the Norm contemplates an affiliate website redirecting players; VOX pushes SMS to its own list.
  • art. 6(3) = closed channel list (numerus clausus) — bonus/promo only via operator site, affiliate websites, or e-messages to the operator's own opted-in active players. A third party SMS-ing its own DB is not on the list.
  • "VOX = delivery pipe" theory defeated — using authorized comms providers "does not change the legal nature" of a third-party direct-marketing campaign.
  • GDPR consent ≠ a legal gambling channel; the redirect link doesn't cure it; no unlicensed escape.

2 Current law (mid-2026)

  • O.U.G. 77/2009 art. 20(6) — connex activities incl. affiliates require an ONJN licence (carve-out for pure electronic-comms providers).
  • H.G. 111/2016 art. 6(3) — closed channel list for bonus promotions + affiliate = website-redirection revenue model.
  • Law 107/2024 — bars gambling-connected services to non-Class-I entities; B2B only to RO-licensed operators.
  • GEO 82/2023 + O.U.G. 77/2009 art. 1² — unsolicited gambling e-messages prohibited; ad tightening.
  • Penalties — promoting unlicensed operators: 50,000–100,000 lei (art. 22(2)/(3)(e)), possible licence revocation (art. 22(4)).

6 Formal Legal Opinion

Opinion of Genie (Opus 4.8, maximum reasoning) · corroborated by the cross-model council (DeepSeek-V4-Pro · GLM-5.2 ×2 · GPT-5.5) · 25 June 2026

RE: Lawfulness under Romanian law of an SMS-based affiliate-marketing model for licensed remote gambling operators (VOX Solutions Europe Ltd / "SMSads"), following ONJN's rejection of the Class-II "marketing afiliat" licence application (decision Nr.19839/19.05.2026).

I. Question presented. Whether VOX/SMSads may lawfully transmit, in Romania, electronic (SMS) commercial communications promoting the services, bonuses or promotions of ONJN-licensed remote gambling operators, to recipients held in VOX's own opt-in database, with embedded redirect links — under a Class-II affiliate licence or any alternative legal basis.

II. Short answer. No. In the form proposed, the activity is impermissible under the special gambling legislation, cannot be authorised by a Class-II affiliate licence, and cannot lawfully be conducted outside the licensing regime. The conclusion is held with high confidence and was independently corroborated by four reviewing models; all concurred in the operative result, with the single dissent (GPT-5.5: "revise, not reverse") directed only to the precision of the reasoning, not the outcome.

III. Governing law. O.U.G. 77/2009 (as amended, incl. GEO 82/2023 and Law 107/2024 — notably art. 1², art. 20(6), and the sanction provisions of art. 22); Methodological Norms approved by H.G. 111/2016 (affiliate definition and art. 6(3)); Law 506/2004 (e-Privacy) and the GDPR for general electronic marketing; Law 554/2004 for administrative challenge.

IV. Analysis.

  • 1 — Closed channel rule (numerus clausus). H.G. 111/2016 art. 6(3) confines promotion of gambling bonuses/promotional actions to a closed list of channels: the operator's own premises, the internet pages of the operator or its affiliates, and electronic messages to players holding active accounts in the operator's own database who have consented. Construed in its systematic context, "own database" denotes the licensed organiser's player database — the only repository in which "active accounts" exist — not a third-party marketer's list. VOX's model (SMS from a third-party list) falls outside every limb. (Refinement: the restriction is, strictly, anchored to bonus/promotional communications; the opinion does not rest on the affiliate definition itself mandating an affiliate-owned website.)
  • 2 — No transformation by intermediation. Authorised electronic-communications providers supply transmission infrastructure only; they cannot convert a third-party direct-marketing campaign into a permitted activity (art. 20(6) expressly distinguishes pure communications providers). The redirect link is a technical attribution device and does not legalise the channel.
  • 3 — Consent is not a substitute. GDPR / e-Privacy opt-in cannot displace the special-law channel restriction; the regimes are cumulative, not alternative.
  • 4 — Retention, not acquisition. Even the only conceivable compliant configuration — VOX as a pure technical sub-processor transmitting on behalf of, and to the database of, a single licensed operator — is limited to players with active accounts, i.e. retention of existing customers. Customer acquisition by SMS is unavailable under any reading, which is fatal to VOX's audience-ownership value proposition.

V. Compliant alternatives (ranked).

  • (a) Class-II affiliate website — a genuine redirecting website (content/SEO, tracking links; bonus promotion shown on its affiliate pages). The licence is not categorically foreclosed; only the SMS-to-own-database use is.
  • (b) Operator-controlled retention SMS — VOX as a sub-processor messaging the operator's own consented active players, under the operator's licence and control (retention only).
  • (c) Non-affiliate creative/media services not remunerated by player participation may avoid "affiliate" characterisation, but remain subject to gambling-advertising, anti-spam and ONJN oversight.
  • (d) Administrative challenge — contestable (Law 554/2004 art. 7: prior complaint within ~30 days of communication; judicial action within ~6 months), but the merits are uphill given the textual barrier; pursue only to preserve optionality on counsel's advice.

VI. Risk. Proceeding as proposed exposes VOX to sanctions for promoting gambling outside permitted channels and — where any promoted operator is unlicensed — fines of 50,000–100,000 lei and possible related measures. Because ONJN has already adjudicated this exact mechanism (Nr.19839), continuation would be knowing and aggravated. The Cyprus seat affords no shield for Romania-targeted promotion. Non-gambling SMS verticals are unaffected and remain available subject to e-Privacy/GDPR consent.

VII. Opinion. It is my opinion that VOX/SMSads should not conduct gambling-promotional SMS to its own database in Romania; should reclassify that activity as prohibited; should pivot Romanian SMS activity to non-gambling verticals; and, if it wishes to retain a Romanian gambling presence, should do so only through a licensed affiliate-website model or operator-controlled retention messaging, on the advice of Romanian gambling counsel.
