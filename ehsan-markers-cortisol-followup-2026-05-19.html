Part 2 — The Cortisol Question (DSIP read-out)

How Bachmeyer measures cortisol

Standard AM serum cortisol + DHEA — he explicitly rejects the complex functional tests:

"Dr. Bachmeyer does NOT use complex functional tests like a 4-point diurnal salivary curve or an ACTH stimulation test. He explicitly advises against obsessing over 'big long fancy lab tests'. Instead he relies on standard blood work to check baseline cortisol and DHEA, but judges adrenal function primarily by symptoms and restoration of the natural circadian rhythm."

The real read-out: symptoms + circadian rhythm

"AM Peak (sharp surge at dawn for drive/waking energy); PM Drop (significant decline at dusk, stays low overnight for deep delta sleep). A 'flattened' curve — lacking morning energy but 'wired but tired' at night — is the ultimate sign of HPA axis dysfunction."
"DSIP directly recalibrates the HPA axis to fix cortisol signaling rather than artificially suppressing it... You know it's working PRIMARILY through symptoms: elimination of 'wired but tired' at night, return of deep uninterrupted sleep, waking with clear energy. If it persists: fragmented sleep, morning exhaustion, high stress."
