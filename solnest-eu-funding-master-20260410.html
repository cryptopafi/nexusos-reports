2. Strategic Pivot — What R1-R8 Changed vs Delphi D4

2.1 France: Rank #1 → Plan B (single blocker)

D4 said: France scores 8.6, Rank #1 Europe, Bourse French Tech + Kima Ventures + ADEME TREMPLIN + Bpifrance matching = Year 1 stack of €80-240K.

R1 found (2026-04-10 PM4):

  1. ADEME TREMPLIN is a phantom program. The name conflates two unrelated programs: ADEME "Tremplin pour la transition écologique des PME" (physical investment, SaaS INELIGIBLE, closed Dec 31 2025) and French Tech Tremplin Bpifrance (diversity incubator, NOT cleantech). D4's France 8.6 score was inflated ~1.5 points by this error.
  2. France 2030 = 75% foreign-ownership cap (verified from lafrenchtech.gouv.fr). A Delaware C-Corp owning 100% of a French SAS is HARD BLOCKED from France 2030. The two-entity strategy D4 recommended is illegal under France 2030.
  3. Bpifrance €500K matching unverifiable for 2026. Cannot confirm current program documentation. Historical "Fonds Business Angels" may be discontinued or renamed.
  4. Bourse French Tech ACTIVE 2026 (€50K standard / €90K Emergence) but foreign ownership rules for 100% Delaware-owned SAS are NOT publicly documented. Single blocking question: email Bpifrance.
  5. Kima Ventures confirmed (€150K SAFE, 2-week decision, cleantech deal Jan 2026) but equity/dilutive.

Revised France stack: ~€200K (€50K conditional grant + €150K equity), not €700K+. France score: 8.6 → ~6.0-6.5.

Verdict: France is a Plan B blocked behind a single Bpifrance email. Not primary Y1 bet.

2.2 Italy: Phase 2 play → Year 1 anchor (landmine debunked)

D4 said: Italian SRL Innovativa may require natural-person ownership under DL 179/2012 Art 25. A Delaware-owned SRL may NOT qualify. Italy = Phase 2 play (month 6+) pending €200-500 legal opinion.

R3 found (2026-04-10 PM4):

  • The DL 179/2012 Art 25 51% natural-person rule DOES NOT EXIST in the current consolidated text. Confirmed by:

- MIMIT Circular 29 July 2025 (most authoritative 2026 source) - MIMIT official Art 25 PDF - Normattiva.it consolidated text - 7/8 Perplexity Sonar Pro sources

  • Real gate is Law 193/2024 Art 28 SME consolidation test (size-based, not nationality-based). SolNest passes easily: Delaware parent = 0 employees, €0 revenue → Italian SRL = micro enterprise independently.
  • Scenario A (Delaware-owned Italian SRL) is VIABLE. Year 1 realistic capture: €300-600K. Legal risk 2/5.
  • Smart&Start Italia 2026 CONFIRMED OPEN (sportello permanente, rolling), €100K-€1.5M at 0% interest rate + 30% non-repayable grant component in Southern Italy (Calabria, Puglia, Sicilia, Basilicata, Campania, Molise, Abruzzo, Sardegna).

Verdict: Italy is the dominant single-jurisdiction Year 1 play for SolNest. Not Phase 2. An optional €1.5-3K legal opinion on SME consolidation calc is nice-to-have, NOT a veto gate.

5. Stack-ability Analysis

5.1 Core rules (EU state aid)

  • Each EU subsidiary receiving aid from its member state is a SEPARATE legal entity/beneficiary.
  • Different subsidiaries receiving aid for activities in their respective countries = NOT double-dipping.
  • EU SME consolidation test (Rec. 2003/361/EC) applies per subsidiary. Delaware parent = 0 employees, €0 revenue → each EU sub qualifies independently as EU micro-enterprise.
  • De minimis ceiling: €300K per entity over 3 rolling fiscal years (cumulated across all de minimis aid from all sources).
  • GBER (General Block Exemption Regulation) programs have higher intensity ceilings than de minimis and can be combined up to eligible cost cap.
  • NL WBSO is classified as a general tax measure, NOT state aid → does NOT count toward de minimis or GBER ceilings.
