Market Overview

Bolivia

Bolivia's peptide market is social-first and storefront-poor. Demand is met through a parallel/grey channel built on Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and WhatsApp groups, plus physical informal markets — La Tablada (La Paz), Calle Tiwanaku (El Alto), and La Ramada (Santa Cruz). Product circulating: BPC-157 injectable, TB-500, GHK-Cu injectable, MOTS-c, Epitalon, NAD+ injectable, tirzepatide/semaglutide. Vendors are ephemeral and DM-based — no durable named entities to affiliate with.

The formal tier: Farmacorp pharmacy chain distributes Tirzec (tirzepatide 10mg, branded "Lipoless") from QUIMFA (Paraguay) — officially registered in Bolivia 2026. AGEMED (Agencia Estatal de Medicamentos y Tecnologías en Salud) is tightening enforcement in 2026. Bolivia is a low-direct-conversion, high-regulatory-risk market requiring an external shipper for any affiliate play.

Ecuador

Ecuador is more organized: at least 3 live local vendor websites with national shipping — peptidosec.com, peptidosecuador.com, mispeptidosec.com — carrying BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu, tirzepatide, retatrutide, semaglutide with CoA (Certificate of Analysis)-verified purity claims. Limitless Biotech (US) is named as upstream supplier. None of the Ecuador locals has a confirmed affiliate program — the closest is peptidosecuador.com's 5% customer signup discount (a discount, NOT an affiliate program).

Domain Cluster Risk
The three *peptidos*ec* domains may share a single operator — WHOIS (domain registration ownership database) unverified. ARCSA (Agencia de Regulación y Control Sanitario) requires Notificación Sanitaria for supplements; GLP-1 biologics require prescription.
