3 · STEP 1 — Corrective protocol (tied to actual findings)

Each item maps to a flagged result. Lead with the non-peptide fix the labs actually demand.

1.1 — Vitamin D repletion (supplement, not a peptide — but the #1 thing the labs require)

  • D3 (cholecalciferol) ~5,000 IU/day + K2 (MK-7) 100–200 µg/day, with fat, 8–12 weeks → re-measure 25-OH-D; maintenance ~2,000–4,000 IU/day to hold 40–60 ng/mL.
  • Why K2: directs calcium to bone (post-menopause) and away from arteries.
  • Safety gate: draw PTH + ionized calcium before high-dose repletion. Exact dose function-based; attending physician sets.

1.2 — CJC-1295 (no-DAC) + Ipamorelin — growth-hormone (GH) secretagogue pair

The one genuinely indicated peptide for a 47-yo post-menopausal woman: addresses age-related GH/IGF-1 decline driving the menopausal shift in body composition, sleep depth, recovery, skin and bone density.

  • CJC-1295 without DAC ~100 µg + Ipamorelin ~100–200 µg, subcutaneous, nightly pre-sleep, empty stomach (≥2 h after food). DAC = Drug Affinity Complex; no-DAC gives a clean natural-rhythm GH pulse.
  • Bachmeyer prefers GH secretagogues over exogenous HGH (human growth hormone), which he says damages the pituitary in ~99.9 % of users.
  • Mandatory baseline + monitor: IGF-1 — currently missing; do not start until drawn. Re-check at ~8–12 weeks (1-week IGF-1 is meaningless per Bachmeyer).

1.3 — MOTS-c — mitochondrial / metabolic

Supports the menopausal metabolic transition; Bachmeyer frames menopause partly as metabolic dysfunction. Her metabolism is already excellent — optimization, not rescue. (Carries into the longevity stack, §4 Pillar B.)

  • ~0.25–0.5 mg/day SC, cycled (e.g. 5 days on / 2 off); take with carbohydrates to avoid hypoglycemia. Monitor fasting insulin + HOMA-IR.

1.4 — Thymosin Alpha-1 — immune (conditional)

For the borderline-low neutrophils / low-normal WBC. Only if a repeat CBC confirms persistent low neutrophils or recurrent infections. Monitor: CBC, symptom-led.

What we deliberately do NOT do for Step 1:
  • No HRT (hormone replacement therapy) push — Bachmeyer treats menopause as metabolic/lifestyle first; estradiol still relatively preserved. HRT is a physician call if symptoms warrant.
  • No statin / LDL intervention — lipids clean, insulin-sensitive.
  • No aggressive DHEA manipulation — only marginally high; interpret with cortisol.

4 · STEP 2 — Full longevity protocol

A complete healthspan stack organized by the biological systems that age. Bachmeyer's thesis: most age-related decline traces to three core failures — systemic inflammation, insulin resistance, and mitochondrial ATP (adenosine triphosphate) shortage — plus telomere attrition, GH-axis decline and immune senescence. Each pillar below targets one. This is a menu, not an "all-at-once" prescription — see the phased rollout at the end. Epitalon (your example) is Pillar A.

Pillar A — Telomere / cellular senescence · Epitalon (Epithalon)

Synthetic pineal tetrapeptide; Bachmeyer's flagship longevity tool. Activates telomerase to slow telomere attrition — the root "clock" of cellular senescence.

  • ~5–10 mg/day subcutaneous, 10–20 consecutive days, cycled every 3–6 months (Russian / Khavinson protocol, Bachmeyer-endorsed).
  • Optional baseline: telomere-length test (e.g. Life Length) for a tracked biomarker.
  • Evidence honesty: human data limited (small Russian studies); mechanism plausible, disease-reversal claims unsubstantiated. Experimental longevity, physician-supervised.

Pillar B — Mitochondrial energy (the engine)

B1 · MOTS-c — metabolic reset

~0.25–0.5 mg/day SC, cycled, with carbs. Insulin sensitization + AMPK activation. Bachmeyer's rule: MOTS-c first as the metabolic reset, SS-31 second. Also "forever-peptide" eligible at low dose.

B2 · SS-31 (Elamipretide) — inner-membrane repair

Tetrapeptide that binds cardiolipin in the inner mitochondrial membrane, stabilizing electron-transport-chain complexes, maximizing ATP and cutting reactive-oxygen-species leakage. Run after the MOTS-c reset.

  • ~5 mg/day SC, cycled (e.g. 4–8 week blocks) — exact dose function-based; physician sets. Symptom-led (energy, recovery).

B3 · NAD+ axis — 5-Amino-1MQ and/or NAD+

5-Amino-1MQ inhibits NNMT (nicotinamide N-methyltransferase) → raises intracellular NAD+, activates sirtuins (SIRT3) / PGC-1α / AMPK. ~50–150 mg/day oral. Optional direct NAD+ ~50–100 mg SC if low energy persists. Monitor: NAD+ level (Bachmeyer tracks this directly).

Pillar C — Growth-hormone axis (decline reversal) · pick ONE strategy

Reverses the age-related GH/IGF-1 fall (body comp, bone, sleep, skin, cognition). Bachmeyer rejects exogenous HGH (pituitary shutdown, insulin resistance, carpal tunnel).

  • Option 1 (default, carries from §1.2): CJC-1295 no-DAC + Ipamorelin — 100 µg + 100–200 µg nightly SC. Clean pulsatile GH.
  • Option 2: Tesamorelin — ~1–2 mg/day SC — stronger on visceral fat + cognition; consider if abdominal fat or brain-fog dominate.
  • GATE: draw IGF-1 first, re-check at 8–12 weeks. Do not stack two GH strategies.

Pillar D — Systemic repair & anti-inflammatory

D1 · BPC-157 — the daily base

~250–500 µg/day SC (Bachmeyer personal ~1 mg/day; don't run 2 mg daily long-term). Gut lining, tendon/joint, vascular, broad anti-inflammatory. Subcutaneous only (oral ineffective). Monitor: hs-CRP (high-sensitivity C-Reactive Protein).

D2 · TB-500 — cyclical tissue/systemic

~2.5–5 mg every ~5 days, cycled on/off. Pairs with BPC-157 for connective-tissue and cardiac/vascular repair. Inject separately (do not mix in one syringe without pH knowledge).

Pillar E — Immune resilience (immunosenescence) · Thymosin Alpha-1

Bachmeyer: "a powerhouse for life." Rebuilds T-cell-mediated immunity that declines with age — doubly relevant given her borderline-low neutrophils. ~1.5 mg SC ~2×/week, cycled. Monitor: CBC + infection frequency.

Pillar F — Skin / gene-expression reset · GHK-Cu

Copper tripeptide; drives a broad "youthful" gene-expression reset, collagen/skin quality, wound and tissue repair (high value in post-menopausal skin). ~1–2 mg/day SC (start ~250–300 µg when stacking). Transient injection-site redness 2–4 days is benign. Injectable only.

Pillar G — Sleep / HPA recalibration · DSIP (conditional)

DSIP = Delta Sleep-Inducing Peptide. Recalibrates the HPA (hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal) axis and restores deep sleep — sleep being the foundation every other longevity lever depends on, and frequently disrupted in menopause. Add only if sleep is poor. ~100–250 µg SC pre-sleep, symptom-led. Bachmeyer judges success by deep-sleep return + clear morning energy.

Foundation — non-peptide supplements (the bedrock)

  • Vitamin D3 + K2 — corrective + permanent (see §1.1).
  • Magnesium ~300–400 mg/day — vit-D cofactor, sleep, glucose (RBC-Mg not measured).
  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA) ~2–3 g/day — anti-inflammatory, cardiovascular, cognitive.
  • Creatine monohydrate ~3–5 g/day — strong evidence for muscle, bone, and cognition in post-menopausal women.
  • Folate: reduce/stop folic-acid supplement (she's high at 33.6); switch to low-dose methyl-folate only if needed.
Recommended phased rollout (physician-gated — don't start everything at once):
  1. Phase 0 — Foundation (weeks 0–4): D3+K2, magnesium, omega-3, creatine. Draw all missing labs (IGF-1, hs-CRP, PTH/iCa, thyroid, iron studies, etc.).
  2. Phase 1 — Well-tolerated daily base (week 2+): BPC-157 + GHK-Cu daily. Start an Epitalon block (10–20 days) anytime.
  3. Phase 2 — Mitochondrial (week 4–6): MOTS-c reset → then SS-31; add 5-Amino-1MQ/NAD+ if energy lags.
  4. Phase 3 — GH axis (after IGF-1 baseline): CJC-1295 no-DAC + Ipamorelin (or Tesamorelin).
  5. Phase 4 — As-needed: Thymosin Alpha-1 (if CBC confirms low neutrophils), TB-500 (recovery blocks), DSIP (if sleep poor).
