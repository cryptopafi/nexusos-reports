3 · STEP 1 — Corrective protocol (tied to actual findings)

Each item maps to a flagged result. Lead with the non-peptide fix that the labs actually demand.

1.1 — Vitamin D repletion (supplement, not a peptide — but it's the #1 thing the labs require)

  • D3 (cholecalciferol) ~5,000 IU/day + K2 (MK-7) 100–200 µg/day, taken with fat, for 8–12 weeks → re-measure 25-OH-D; maintenance ~2,000–4,000 IU/day to hold 40–60 ng/mL.
  • Why K2: Bachmeyer pairs D3 with K2 to direct calcium to bone (post-menopausal relevance) and away from arteries.
  • Safety gate: draw PTH + ionized calcium before high-dose repletion (missing-lab list). Exact dose = function-based; attending physician sets.
  • Adjunct: magnesium (cofactor for vit-D activation) — RBC magnesium not measured; consider 300–400 mg/day.

1.2 — CJC-1295 (no-DAC) + Ipamorelin — growth-hormone (GH) secretagogue pair

The one genuinely indicated peptide optimization for a 47-yo post-menopausal woman: addresses the age-related GH/IGF-1 decline that drives the menopausal shift in body composition, sleep depth, recovery, skin and bone density. Bachmeyer's core "four-peptide longevity" backbone.

  • CJC-1295 without DAC ~100 µg + Ipamorelin ~100–200 µg, subcutaneous, nightly pre-sleep on an empty stomach (≥2 h after food). DAC = Drug Affinity Complex (the no-DAC form gives a clean GH pulse mimicking natural rhythm).
  • Bachmeyer prefers GH secretagogues over exogenous HGH (human growth hormone), which he says damages the pituitary in ~99.9 % of users.
  • Mandatory baseline + monitor: IGF-1 (Insulin-like Growth Factor 1) — currently missing; do not start until drawn. Re-check IGF-1 at ~8–12 weeks (1-week IGF-1 is meaningless per Bachmeyer).

1.3 — MOTS-c — mitochondrial / metabolic

Supports the menopausal metabolic transition and mitochondrial efficiency; Bachmeyer frames menopause partly as metabolic dysfunction. Her metabolism is already excellent, so this is optimization, not rescue.

  • Cycled (Bachmeyer also calls it a "forever peptide" for continuous low-dose use). Take with carbohydrates to avoid hypoglycemia.
  • Monitor: fasting insulin + HOMA-IR (already optimal — confirm they stay there).

1.4 — Thymosin Alpha-1 — immune (optional, low priority)

For the borderline-low neutrophils / low-normal WBC and general immune resilience. Bachmeyer: "a powerhouse for life." Discretionary — only if a repeat CBC confirms persistently low neutrophils or she has recurrent infections.

  • Monitor: CBC; symptom-led.
What we deliberately do NOT do for Step 1:
  • No hormone replacement therapy (HRT) push — Bachmeyer's "menopause doesn't need HRT" stance treats it as a metabolic/lifestyle problem first; estradiol is still relatively preserved here. HRT is a physician decision if symptoms warrant.
  • No statin / LDL intervention — lipids are clean and she's insulin-sensitive.
  • No aggressive DHEA manipulation — it's only marginally high; interpret with cortisol, don't chase it.
  • Kisspeptin (sex-hormone-axis stimulation) is held — discretionary/advanced, and not justified without LH + a clear goal.

4 · STEP 2 — Longevity protocol (not tied to a specific abnormality)

2.1 — Epitalon (Epithalon) — Bachmeyer's #1 longevity peptide

Synthetic pineal tetrapeptide; positioned by Bachmeyer as the single most important anti-aging tool — activates telomerase to slow telomere attrition (the root "clock" of cellular senescence).

  • Dose (Russian / Khavinson protocol, Bachmeyer-endorsed): ~5–10 mg/day subcutaneous, 10–20 consecutive days, cycled every 3–6 months.
  • Optional baseline: telomere-length test (e.g. Life Length) if she wants a tracked biomarker — otherwise symptom/age-based.
  • Evidence honesty: human data is limited (mostly small Russian studies); mechanism is plausible, claims of disease reversal are not substantiated. Frame as experimental longevity, physician-supervised.

2.2 — GHK-Cu (copper tripeptide) — skin, gene-regulation, tissue

  • ~1–2 mg/day subcutaneous (start lower, ~250–300 µg, when stacking). Drives broad "youthful" gene-expression reset, collagen/skin, wound and tissue repair.
  • Note: transient injection-site redness for 2–4 days is common/benign. Oral route is ineffective (peptidase degradation) — injectable only.

2.3 — BPC-157 — systemic repair / gut / anti-inflammatory

  • ~250–500 µg/day subcutaneous as a maintenance "forever" peptide (Bachmeyer's personal protocol ~1 mg/day; do not run 2 mg daily long-term).
  • Gut-lining, tendon/joint, vascular and anti-inflammatory support. Subcutaneous only (oral ineffective).
  • Monitor: hs-CRP (high-sensitivity C-Reactive Protein) — currently missing; draw it as the inflammation baseline.

2.4 — MOTS-c & (optional) NAD+ optimization — mitochondrial longevity

MOTS-c carries over from Step 1.3 as a longevity "forever peptide." NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) support is the mitochondrial-energy adjunct Bachmeyer monitors directly (NAD+ level). Sequence MOTS-c as the metabolic reset before any SS-31 (Elamipretide) consideration later.

Suggested longevity sequencing (physician-gated):
  1. Foundation (weeks 0–8): Vitamin D3+K2 repletion + draw all missing labs. BPC-157 + GHK-Cu daily (well-tolerated, no hormonal axis effect).
  2. Add GH axis (week 4–8, after IGF-1 baseline): CJC-1295 no-DAC + Ipamorelin nightly. MOTS-c cycled.
  3. Epitalon block (any time, standalone): 5–10 mg/day × 10–20 days, then off; repeat every 3–6 months.
